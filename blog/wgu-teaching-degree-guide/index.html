WGU Teaching Degrees: Become a Licensed Teacher Online

December 21, 2025 10 min read Degree Guides

WGU teaching degrees are designed for working adults who want to earn a credential online while preparing for licensure. The key decision is whether you need a licensure track (to teach in K-12 classrooms) or a non-licensure education degree. This guide breaks down the differences, clinical requirements, and the best way to plan your timeline.

WGU teaching degrees at a glance

WGU offers online education programs that are competency-based, meaning you progress by demonstrating mastery. Programs include bachelor’s and master’s options aligned to classroom teaching. WGU’s Teachers College is accredited by CAEP, and some programs also hold AAQEP accreditation.

  • Online, competency-based format with flexible pacing
  • Licensure and non-licensure pathways available
  • Strong focus on classroom-ready skills and practical teaching

Licensure vs. non-licensure pathways

Licensure eligibility depends on the state where you plan to teach. WGU notes that licensure requirements vary by state and often include background checks, exams (commonly Praxis), and required classroom hours.

Tip: Check your state’s requirements early so you can plan timelines for exams, fingerprints, and field placements.

If you do not need a teaching license, WGU also offers non-licensure education degrees that emphasize leadership, curriculum, or instructional design.

Clinical experience and student teaching

WGU requires real classroom practice. Students complete clinical experience (a minimum of 65 hours) before student teaching. Student teaching requires a minimum of 60 days in a classroom setting and includes demonstration teaching.

These requirements are in-person, so plan ahead with your mentor and local school placement to avoid scheduling delays.

What you will study

Coursework typically emphasizes practical teaching skills alongside subject-area methods:

  • Lesson planning and instructional design
  • Classroom management and student engagement
  • Assessment strategies and data-informed instruction
  • Differentiation and inclusive practices
  • Educational technology for modern classrooms

How to finish faster

WGU is self-paced, which makes acceleration possible if you plan carefully:

  1. Start with transfer credits to reduce general education requirements.
  2. Use rubric-first strategies for performance assessments.
  3. Schedule consistent study blocks each week.
  4. Coordinate early with your mentor for field placement timelines.

Helpful next reads: WGU Study Tips for Working Adults and WGU Capstone Project Guide.

Career paths after graduation

Licensure programs lead to classroom teaching roles, while non-licensure tracks support broader education careers. Common outcomes include:

  • K-12 classroom teacher (elementary, secondary, or special education)
  • Instructional coach or curriculum specialist
  • Training and development or instructional design roles
