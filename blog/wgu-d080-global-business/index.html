WGU D080 Global Business: How to Pass This Challenging Course

December 18, 2025 7 min read Course Help

WGU D080 (Global Business) can feel “hard” because it’s not about memorizing one chapter at a time—it’s about applying a few core ideas to many different scenarios. Once you understand the frameworks (culture, strategy, trade, and entry modes), the course becomes much more predictable.

Fastest path: Study frameworks → practice applying them to short scenarios → review mistakes → repeat. Reading the full text repeatedly is slower than targeted application practice.

What WGU D080 Covers (and What the OA Tests)

D080 is usually built around the decisions companies make when they operate across borders: where to expand, how to enter a market, how culture affects management, and how trade/currency/politics shape risk.

Most questions are scenario-based. You’ll be asked to choose the best action given a context—so your goal is to recognize the framework that applies and eliminate wrong answers quickly.

Important: Course versions and assessments can change. Use your coaching report and course resources as your “truth,” and treat this guide as a study strategy—not a substitute.

Core Concepts to Master for Global Business

1) Culture and Communication

Culture frameworks help you predict how communication, leadership, and negotiation differ across regions. When you see a scenario about misunderstandings, teamwork, feedback style, or hierarchy, think “culture framework.”

  • High-context vs low-context: how direct communication is, and how much meaning is implied.
  • Power distance: how comfortable people are with hierarchy and authority.
  • Individualism vs collectivism: whether decisions are individual-first or group-first.

2) Global Strategy Basics

D080 often blends global business with strategy fundamentals. If a question asks “What should the company do next?” you’re in strategy territory.

  • SWOT: internal strengths/weaknesses and external opportunities/threats.
  • Porter-style thinking: competition, substitutes, buyer/supplier power, and barriers.
  • Standardization vs localization: one global approach vs adapting to local needs.

3) Trade, Currency, and Risk

Global business decisions are shaped by costs, regulation, politics, and exchange rates. You don’t need deep finance math, but you do need to understand the direction of impact.

  • Tariffs/quotas: raise costs and change pricing strategy.
  • Currency risk: exchange rate swings can turn “profit” into “loss” if unmanaged.
  • Country risk: stability, regulation, and legal environment affect entry choices.

4) Market Entry Modes

Entry modes show up constantly because they combine risk, control, speed, and cost.

  • Exporting: lower risk, lower control, often slower learning.
  • Licensing/franchising: faster expansion, less operational control.
  • Joint venture: shared risk and local expertise, but potential conflicts.
  • Wholly owned subsidiary: maximum control, higher risk/cost.

A Focused Study Plan to Pass D080

If you want to accelerate, keep it structured. Use this as a baseline and adjust based on your coaching report.

  1. Day 1: Skim the course outline, then write a one-page framework sheet (culture + entry modes).
  2. Day 2: Strategy tools (SWOT, competition, standardize vs localize) + short scenario practice.
  3. Day 3: Trade/currency/politics risk + practice “cause and effect” questions.
  4. Day 4: Mixed practice sets + review mistakes, update your one-pager.
  5. Day 5: Final review + timed practice + schedule the OA if your scores are stable.
Pro Tip: For each missed question, write (1) the framework you should have used and (2) the clue in the scenario that should have triggered it. That single habit reduces repeat mistakes fast.

How to Practice Like the OA

Because D080 is scenario-heavy, your practice should be scenario-heavy. When you review, avoid just memorizing the “right answer.” Instead, ask:

  • What framework was being tested?
  • What words in the scenario were the clue?
  • Why are the other answers wrong (not just why the right one is right)?

If you’re also working through an MBA track, connect D080 concepts to real business situations you’ve seen—this makes recall easier and improves application.
