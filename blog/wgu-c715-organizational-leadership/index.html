WGU C715 Organizational Leadership: Graduate Course Guide

December 24, 2025 7 min read Course Help

WGU C715 Organizational Leadership is about making leadership decisions that improve culture, performance, and change outcomes. The course focuses on how leaders influence people and systems, not just theory definitions. This guide summarizes the core topics and gives you a clear prep path for the OA.

For program context, see WGU MBA Program: Complete Guide. For exam strategy, review How to Pass WGU Objective Assessments.

Official course focus (WGU catalog)

The WGU Institutional Catalog describes C715 as a leadership-focused course that emphasizes organizational leadership, ethics, culture, and change management.

Core competencies to master

  • Apply leadership theories to real organizational challenges
  • Lead change with clear communication and stakeholder alignment
  • Build a culture that supports performance and accountability
  • Use ethical frameworks for decision-making
  • Manage conflict and improve team dynamics

Leadership theories you should know

  • Transformational: inspire and align people to a vision
  • Transactional: manage through goals and rewards
  • Servant: remove barriers and develop teams
  • Situational: adapt style to readiness and skill

Ethical leadership and trust

Ethics questions are common in C715. Choose the option that protects stakeholders, builds trust, and aligns to organizational values. Short-term wins that damage credibility are rarely correct.

Change management basics

Leaders are responsible for guiding people through change. Focus on communication, stakeholder buy-in, and clear milestones. The best answers usually include a plan to reduce resistance and reinforce adoption.

Tip: If a scenario mentions resistance, the best response usually includes communication, training, and involvement, not just enforcement.

Culture and organizational alignment

Culture is shaped by what leaders reward, measure, and model. When you see low morale or performance issues, the right answer often changes incentives, expectations, and accountability.

Leadership communication

Clear communication is the fastest way to fix confusion and misalignment. In scenario questions, choose options that clarify expectations and create feedback loops.

Decision-making frameworks

Leaders balance data, ethics, and people impact. If you can explain why a decision fits the organization values and goals, you are likely on the right track.

Study plan (2 to 3 weeks)

  1. Week 1: Leadership theories and ethical frameworks.
  2. Week 2: Change management, culture, and communication.
  3. Week 3: Scenario practice and targeted review.

If you only have one week

  1. Days 1-2: Leadership theory quick review.
  2. Days 3-4: Change management and culture.
  3. Days 5-6: Scenario questions and decision frameworks.
  4. Day 7: Final review and checklist.

Assessment-day strategy

  • Identify the leadership challenge first
  • Choose the option that improves long-term trust and performance
  • Reject short-term fixes that ignore culture or ethics

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Choosing the harshest option instead of the most sustainable
  • Ignoring communication when change fails
  • Applying the wrong leadership style to the situation

Ready-to-test checklist

  • I can connect each scenario to a leadership theory
  • I can explain how culture influences performance
  • I can choose ethical responses that build trust
