How to Accelerate Through WGU: Complete Your Degree Faster

December 16, 2025 10 min read Study Tips

WGU's competency-based model is designed for acceleration. Unlike traditional universities where you're locked into semester schedules, WGU lets you move as fast as you can demonstrate mastery. Some students complete their bachelor's degrees in under a year, while others finish master's programs in just six months.

This guide covers the strategies that the fastest WGU students use to accelerate through their degrees while maintaining quality learning.

Why Acceleration Makes Sense

WGU charges a flat tuition rate per 6-month term—around $4,000-$4,500 depending on your program. Whether you complete 12 credits or 60 credits in that term, you pay the same amount. This creates a powerful financial incentive to accelerate:

  • Save thousands in tuition: Finishing in 2 terms instead of 4 saves $8,000-$9,000
  • Enter the workforce sooner: Every term saved is 6 months of potential income
  • Career advancement: Get that promotion or salary increase faster
  • Reduced opportunity cost: Less time balancing school with work and family

Maximize Your Transfer Credits

The fastest path to graduation is entering with as many transfer credits as possible. WGU accepts up to 75% of your degree requirements through transfer.

Sources of Transfer Credit

  • Previous college coursework: Credits from regionally accredited institutions
  • Industry certifications: CompTIA, Cisco, AWS, and many others count as course credit
  • Military training: ACE-evaluated military courses and training
  • CLEP and DSST exams: Standardized tests that earn college credit
  • Prior Learning Assessment: Demonstrate competency through portfolio review
Pro Tip: Before enrolling, work with a WGU Enrollment Counselor to get an unofficial transfer evaluation. This helps you understand exactly how many credits will transfer and plan accordingly.

Certifications That Transfer

For IT and business students, certifications can knock out significant credit requirements:

  • CompTIA A+, Network+, Security+: Each can transfer as 1-2 course credits
  • Cisco CCNA: Often counts for multiple networking courses
  • AWS Cloud Practitioner: Transfers for cloud computing courses
  • Project Management Professional (PMP): Covers project management requirements
  • Six Sigma certifications: Transfer for operations/quality courses

Strategic Term Planning

Successful accelerators don't just jump in randomly—they plan their terms strategically.

Front-Load Easy Courses

Start your term with courses where you have existing knowledge or that are known to be faster. This builds momentum and confidence:

  • Take pre-assessments for all your courses before the term starts (if allowed)
  • Identify courses you might already be able to pass with minimal study
  • Complete quick wins first to build momentum
  • Save challenging courses for when you've got your rhythm

Batch Similar Courses

Group courses by subject matter when possible. If you're taking multiple finance courses, study them in sequence while the concepts are fresh:

  • Related courses share common concepts and terminology
  • Study materials often overlap between related courses
  • Context switching between unrelated subjects wastes mental energy
Important: Some courses have prerequisites or must be taken in a certain order. Work with your Program Mentor to understand any constraints before planning your term.

Maximizing Study Efficiency

Acceleration isn't about studying more hours—it's about studying smarter. Here's how successful accelerators maximize their efficiency:

The Pre-Assessment First Approach

For OA courses, take the pre-assessment immediately when you start the course, even if you know nothing. This tells you:

  • Exactly which competencies you need to study
  • Which areas you already know (possibly from prior experience)
  • How far you are from being ready for the actual exam

For detailed strategies on passing Objective Assessments, see our WGU OA Exam Tips guide.

Skip What You Know

WGU's competency model means you don't need to read every page of every textbook. If you already know a topic:

  • Take the pre-assessment to confirm your knowledge
  • Focus only on weak areas identified in the coaching report
  • Move directly to the OA when you're ready

Use the Right Resources

The official course materials aren't always the most efficient path. Successful accelerators often use:

  • Cohort videos: Condensed review of all competencies
  • Quizlet flashcards: Course-specific sets from other students
  • YouTube explanations: For difficult concepts that need visual explanation
  • Reddit r/WGU: Course-specific tips and study strategies

Time Management for Working Adults

Most WGU students are working adults with jobs and families. Acceleration requires finding and protecting study time:

Calculate Your Available Hours

Be realistic about how much time you can dedicate to studying each week:

  • Minimum viable: 15-20 hours/week for moderate progress
  • Acceleration pace: 25-35 hours/week for fast progress
  • Maximum acceleration: 40+ hours/week for aggressive timelines

Find Hidden Time

Successful accelerators find time others miss:

  • Commute time: Listen to recorded lectures or review notes
  • Lunch breaks: Even 30 minutes of daily study adds up
  • Early mornings: Study before the family wakes up
  • Waiting time: Use phone apps for flashcards while waiting

Protect Your Study Time

Treat study time as non-negotiable appointments:

  • Block specific hours on your calendar
  • Communicate boundaries to family and friends
  • Create a dedicated study space free from distractions
  • Use website blockers during study sessions

Tackling Performance Assessments

Performance Assessments (PAs) are written assignments rather than exams. They can be trickier for acceleration because:

  • They require original work that takes time to produce
  • Grading turnaround is 3-5 days, which can slow momentum
  • Revisions may be required if you don't meet competency

PA Acceleration Strategies

  • Read the rubric first: Understand exactly what evaluators are looking for
  • Use the template: Most PAs have a suggested structure—follow it
  • Don't over-write: Meet the requirements without excessive padding
  • Submit early in the week: Avoid weekend grading delays
  • Start the next course while waiting: Don't lose momentum during grading
Pro Tip: While waiting for a PA to be graded, start the pre-assessment for your next OA course. This keeps you productive during the waiting period.

Avoiding Burnout

Acceleration is a marathon, not a sprint. Pushing too hard leads to burnout, which ultimately slows you down:

  • Schedule rest days: Take at least one day per week completely off
  • Celebrate milestones: Acknowledge each completed course
  • Stay connected: Don't isolate yourself completely
  • Monitor your energy: Reduce intensity if you feel overwhelmed
  • Remember your why: Keep your goals visible for motivation

Working With Your Program Mentor

Your Program Mentor is your partner in acceleration. Build a productive relationship:

  • Be proactive: Reach out before problems arise
  • Share your goals: Let them know you want to accelerate
  • Ask about course order: They know which sequences work best
  • Request exam unlocks: When you're ready, don't wait for scheduled calls
  • Provide updates: Keep them informed of your progress
