Study.com for WGU: Maximize Transfer Credits and Save Money

December 18, 2025 10 min read Degree Planning

Study.com WGU is a popular route for students who want transfer credits beyond what Sophia offers. Study.com can be especially useful when your program requires a specific course that isn’t available on Sophia—or when you prefer video-first lessons and structured quizzes.

This guide breaks down how Study.com works, what it costs, how exams are handled, how to send transcripts to WGU, and the simplest way to use Study.com without wasting money.

High-level strategy: Use Sophia for broad general education first, then use Study.com for the remaining transferable courses Sophia doesn’t cover.

Study.com for WGU: what it is

Study.com offers online courses that can be credit-recommended through ACE or NCCRS. Students typically complete lessons and quizzes throughout the course, then pass a final exam (rules can vary by course).

WGU transfer credit is program-specific, so your goal is to pick Study.com courses that map to your exact WGU degree requirements.

Pricing & course limits (what you actually pay for)

Study.com lists different subscription plans. For college credit, Study.com lists a College Saver plan at $235/month.

Study.com also states that College Saver includes the opportunity to earn up to two college credits per month, and that additional exams can cost $70 each (always confirm current rules, because policies can change).

  • What College Saver includes (Study.com Support)

How Study.com courses work (typical structure)

Exact requirements vary by course, but a common pattern is:

  1. Watch short video lessons (or read lesson summaries).
  2. Complete quizzes as you go.
  3. Complete any required assignments (if the course has them).
  4. Pass a final exam.

If you learn well from structured content, Study.com can feel more guided than pure self-study.

Exams & identity verification (proctored vs non-proctored)

Study.com indicates that many credit-recommended courses use a non-proctored, open-book final exam with identity verification and a time limit (for example, 120 minutes). Rules can vary by course and can change over time, so verify the policy for your specific course before you plan your month.

Source: Credit recommended course changes (Study.com Support).

Transcripts & WGU deadlines

Two transcript rules matter:

  • Study.com transcripts: follow Study.com’s transcript request process for college credit courses.
  • WGU deadlines: WGU transcript guidance states transcripts should be received by the 5th of the month prior to your start date.
  • Study.com transcript request instructions
  • WGU transcript request guidance
Don’t start WGU too early: If your transfer credits aren’t evaluated before your term starts, you may end up taking courses you could have transferred in.

Best strategy for WGU students (simple and cost-efficient)

If your goal is minimizing cost, the strategy is straightforward:

  1. Transfer the easy wins first: Sophia is usually the best starting point. See our guide: Sophia Learning for WGU.
  2. Use Study.com for the gaps: choose Study.com courses specifically because they map to remaining WGU requirements.
  3. Plan around the monthly cap: if your plan includes two college credits per month, don’t subscribe for a month when you can’t finish them.
  4. Send transcripts early: build in buffer time for processing before your WGU start date.

For the big-picture framework, start here: WGU Transfer Credits Guide.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Choosing courses without mapping: only take Study.com courses you can verify will transfer to your WGU program.
  • Not planning the month: subscribe when you can actually finish the courses included in your plan.
  • Ignoring transcript deadlines: build buffer time; don’t assume last-minute processing will work.
