How to Pass WGU Objective Assessments: Proven OA Study Strategies

December 16, 2025 8 min read Exam Preparation

Objective Assessments (OAs) are the backbone of WGU's competency-based education model. Unlike traditional college exams, OAs test whether you truly understand and can apply the material—not just memorize it. For many WGU students, especially those balancing work and family, passing OAs efficiently is the key to accelerating through their degree.

This guide covers proven strategies that successful WGU students use to pass their Objective Assessments on the first attempt, saving time and reducing stress.

Understanding WGU Objective Assessments

Before diving into strategies, it's important to understand what makes WGU OAs different from traditional exams:

  • Competency-based: You're not graded on a curve. You either demonstrate competency or you don't.
  • Application-focused: Questions test your ability to apply concepts to real-world scenarios, not just recall facts.
  • Proctored: All OAs are taken under webcam supervision using proctoring software.
  • No partial credit: You need to meet the competency threshold to pass.

Master the Pre-Assessment Strategy

The Pre-Assessment (PA) is your most powerful tool for OA preparation. Here's how to use it effectively:

Take the PA Early

Don't wait until you feel "ready" to take the pre-assessment. Take it as soon as you start the course, even if you know nothing about the material. The coaching report will show you exactly what competencies you need to focus on.

Pro Tip: The PA mirrors the OA in format and difficulty. Many students report the actual OA feeling similar to—or even easier than—the PA.

Analyze Your Coaching Report

After completing the PA, you'll receive a detailed coaching report. This report is gold. It tells you:

  • Which competency areas you're strong in
  • Which areas need improvement
  • Specific topics to study
Important: If you don't pass the PA, don't immediately retake it. Study the weak areas first. Retaking without studying just wastes an attempt and gives you false confidence.

Effective Study Strategies

Focus on Understanding, Not Memorization

WGU OA questions are application-style questions. You'll be given scenarios and asked to apply concepts. Simply memorizing definitions won't work. You need to:

  • Understand why concepts work the way they do
  • Practice applying concepts to different scenarios
  • Connect related concepts together

Use WGU's Built-In Resources

WGU provides excellent resources that many students overlook:

  • Cohorts: Join the "Competency Review" cohorts that include videos for each competency on the OA
  • Boot Camps: Monthly live events where instructors cover the most commonly missed topics
  • Course Instructor Videos: Watch all mentor and instructor videos—they often highlight what's most important
  • Student Success Center: Test-taking strategies and study skills resources

Leverage External Resources

Beyond WGU's materials, successful students often use:

  • Quizlet: Find or create flashcard sets for your specific course
  • YouTube: Search for explanations of difficult concepts
  • Reddit r/WGU: Course-specific tips from students who recently passed
  • WGU Discord servers: Real-time help from fellow students

Test-Taking Tips for OA Day

Prepare Your Environment

Before your proctored exam:

  • Clear your desk of everything except your computer and ID
  • Ensure good lighting so the proctor can see you clearly
  • Test your webcam and microphone beforehand
  • Have your state or federal ID ready
  • Close all other programs and browser tabs

Technical Preparation

Technical issues are the biggest source of test-day stress. Prevent them by:

  • Testing the Guardian Browser or proctoring software before exam day
  • Using a reliable external webcam if your built-in one is low quality
  • Having a stable internet connection (use ethernet if possible)
  • Knowing how to contact live tech support if issues arise

During the Exam

  • Read carefully: Application questions often have subtle differences in wording that change the correct answer
  • Eliminate wrong answers: Even if you're unsure, eliminating obviously wrong options improves your odds
  • Flag and return: Don't get stuck on one question. Flag it and come back after answering easier ones
  • Manage your time: Keep track of how many questions remain and how much time you have

What If You Don't Pass?

First, don't panic. Failing an OA isn't the end of the world, and WGU has a clear process for retakes:

  1. Your Course Instructor will contact you with a personalized study plan
  2. Complete the recommended study activities
  3. Your instructor will re-open the exam when they determine you're ready
  4. There's no additional charge for retakes (up to 4 attempts total)
Remember: WGU allows up to four attempts per OA. Use each failed attempt as a learning opportunity. Review what you missed and focus your studying on those areas.

Connecting OAs to Degree Acceleration

Passing OAs efficiently is crucial for accelerating through your WGU degree. Here's how OA mastery connects to faster completion:

  • First-time passes save time: Every retake delays your progress by days or weeks
  • Confidence builds momentum: Passing OAs consistently keeps you motivated
  • Better time estimates: Understanding your OA readiness helps you plan your term
