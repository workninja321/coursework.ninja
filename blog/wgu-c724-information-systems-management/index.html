WGU C724: Information Systems Management Study Guide

December 21, 2025 7 min read Course Help

WGU C724 (Information Systems Management) helps you connect IT decisions to business outcomes. If you can explain how systems enable strategy, security, and operations, you are already on the right track.

Course overview

This course emphasizes how organizations plan, govern, and manage information systems. Expect questions that tie technology choices to business value.

Core IS management concepts

  • Information systems components (people, process, technology)
  • Business processes and workflow optimization
  • Enterprise systems (ERP, CRM, SCM)
  • Data management, reporting, and analytics
  • Security, risk, and compliance fundamentals

Strategy alignment and governance

A large portion of C724 focuses on alignment. You should be able to explain how IT supports competitive advantage, how governance structures reduce risk, and why project selection matters.

Tip: When in doubt, choose the option that best aligns IT decisions with organizational goals.

Assessment strategy

C724 typically uses an objective assessment. Build your plan around understanding terminology and applying it to scenarios:

  1. Take the pre-assessment early.
  2. Review weak domains and build a study glossary.
  3. Use practice questions to reinforce scenario thinking.

Study plan

  1. Days 1–3: Read course materials and build vocabulary
  2. Days 4–6: Focus on governance, security, and infrastructure
  3. Days 7–9: Review enterprise systems and strategy alignment
  4. Days 10–12: Practice scenario questions and finalize review

Exam-day tips

  • Read each scenario carefully before choosing an answer
  • Look for alignment between IT and business objectives
  • Eliminate options that ignore governance or risk controls
