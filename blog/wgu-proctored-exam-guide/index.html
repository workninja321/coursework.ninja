WGU Proctored Exam Guide: Meazure Learning, Requirements & Tips for Success

December 17, 2025 10 min read Study Tips

WGU's Objective Assessments (OAs) are proctored exams that verify your identity and ensure academic integrity. While the proctoring process might seem intimidating at first, understanding what to expect makes it straightforward.

This guide covers everything you need to know: the proctoring platform, technical requirements, room setup, what happens during the exam, and tips to ensure a smooth experience.

Proctoring Overview

WGU uses Meazure Learning (formerly Examity) for online proctoring. Here's how it works:

  • Guardian Browser: Specialized browser that locks down your computer during exams
  • Live Proctoring: A human proctor monitors your exam via webcam
  • AI Monitoring: Software flags suspicious behavior for proctor review
  • Identity Verification: Government-issued ID required before each exam
  • Room Scan: 360-degree webcam scan of your testing environment

The process is designed to replicate the security of an in-person testing center while allowing you to test from home.

Technical Requirements

Before scheduling your first exam, ensure your setup meets these requirements:

Computer Requirements

  • Operating System: Windows 10/11, macOS 10.14+, or Chrome OS
  • Processor: 2.0 GHz dual-core or better
  • RAM: 4 GB minimum (8 GB recommended)
  • Hard Drive: 1 GB free space for Guardian Browser
  • Display: 1024x768 resolution minimum

Internet & Peripherals

  • Internet Speed: 1 Mbps minimum (3+ Mbps recommended)
  • Connection: Ethernet preferred over WiFi for stability
  • Webcam: Built-in or external, minimum 640x480 resolution
  • Microphone: Built-in or external (required for proctor communication)
  • Speakers: Required to hear proctor instructions
Pro Tip: Run the system check at least 24 hours before your scheduled exam. This gives you time to resolve any technical issues. Access the system check through your WGU student portal.

Guardian Browser Installation

  1. Download Guardian Browser from the WGU Assessment Portal
  2. Install the application (administrator privileges required)
  3. Restart your computer after installation
  4. Run a test session to verify webcam, microphone, and screen sharing work
  5. Keep the browser updated—new versions are released regularly

Room Setup Requirements

Your testing environment must meet specific criteria. Proctors will flag or stop exams if requirements aren't met.

Room Setup Checklist
  • Private room with a closable door
  • Room must be well-lit (no backlighting behind you)
  • Desk/table clear of all materials except computer
  • No dual monitors (disconnect second monitor)
  • No smartwatches, phones, or tablets in the room
  • No other people present in the room
  • No TVs, virtual assistants, or smart speakers active
  • Mirror or reflective surfaces covered
  • Whiteboards wiped clean
  • Books and notes removed or covered

What You CAN Have

  • Plain paper and pencil: For scratch work (proctor will ask to see it's blank)
  • Water in clear container: No labels or other items
  • Calculator: Only if explicitly allowed for that exam (proctor will verify)
  • Headphones/earbuds: Only if you have an approved accommodation

Room Scan Process

Before the exam starts, the proctor will ask you to:

  1. Show your government-issued ID to the webcam
  2. Pan the webcam around the entire room (360 degrees)
  3. Show your desk surface and under the desk
  4. Show both sides of any scratch paper (must be blank)
  5. Show your ears (to confirm no earbuds)
Common Issue: If you're using a laptop with a built-in webcam, the room scan can be awkward. Consider a USB webcam you can easily move around, or be prepared to pick up your laptop carefully to show the room.

Exam Day Process

Here's exactly what happens when you take a proctored exam:

Before the Exam

  1. 15 minutes early: Log into Guardian Browser
  2. Close all applications: Guardian will prompt you to close running programs
  3. Connect with proctor: Wait in queue (usually 2-10 minutes)
  4. ID verification: Show your government ID to the webcam
  5. Room scan: Complete the 360-degree environment check
  6. Rules review: Proctor explains exam rules
  7. Begin exam: Proctor releases the exam to you

During the Exam

  • Stay in webcam view: Your face must remain visible at all times
  • No leaving: You cannot leave the room during the exam (no bathroom breaks)
  • Talk through problems aloud: Mumbling is okay, but excessive silence may be flagged
  • Don't cover your mouth: Proctors watch for signs of reading questions aloud
  • No reading questions out loud: This is considered a security violation
  • Keep eyes on screen: Frequently looking away may be flagged

After the Exam

  1. Click "Submit" when finished (don't just close the browser)
  2. Wait for proctor confirmation that the exam is submitted
  3. Show scratch paper to the webcam (both sides)
  4. Tear up scratch paper on camera
  5. Proctor ends the session

Scheduling Your Exam

WGU offers flexible scheduling with exams available 24/7. Here's how to schedule:

When Can You Schedule?

  • Availability: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
  • Advance booking: Schedule up to 30 days in advance
  • Same-day exams: Available if slots are open (not guaranteed)
  • Peak times: Evenings and weekends fill up fastest

Scheduling Tips

  • Schedule when you're mentally sharpest (morning person vs. night owl)
  • Avoid scheduling right before or after meals
  • Book at least 2-3 days in advance for best time selection
  • Leave buffer time before any other commitments
  • Consider time zone differences if traveling

Rescheduling & Cancellation

  • Free cancellation: More than 24 hours before scheduled time
  • Late cancellation: Within 24 hours counts as an attempt (in some cases)
  • No-show: May count as a failed attempt

Attempts and Retakes

Understanding the attempt policy helps you plan your study strategy:

Attempt Limits

  • Maximum attempts: 4 per Objective Assessment
  • Wait period: Minimum 24 hours between attempts (often longer required)
  • Remediation: Required activities between attempts
  • Mentor approval: May be required before subsequent attempts

What Counts as an Attempt?

  • Starting the exam (even if you don't finish)
  • Technical issues that prevent completion (proctor will document)
  • Academic integrity violations (automatic failure)

What Doesn't Count?

  • Technical issues before the exam starts
  • Canceled exams (with proper notice)
  • Proctor-approved technical restarts
Important: If you fail all 4 attempts, you may need to take a different version of the course or complete additional remediation. Work closely with your Program Mentor to avoid this situation.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Before the Exam

  • Guardian won't install: Try running as administrator, disable antivirus temporarily
  • Webcam not detected: Check privacy settings, try a different USB port
  • Long wait times: Schedule during off-peak hours (weekday mornings)
  • System check fails: Update browser, check firewall settings

During the Exam

  • Internet disconnects: Stay calm, proctor will attempt to reconnect
  • Screen freezes: Don't close browser—contact proctor via chat
  • Proctor is unresponsive: Use chat feature, be patient
  • Can't hear proctor: Check volume, ask them to type in chat

After Issues

  • Technical failure: Document what happened, contact Assessment Services
  • Disputed violation: Contact your Program Mentor immediately
  • Score not appearing: Allow 24-48 hours, then contact Assessment Services

Certification Exams (External Proctoring)

Some WGU courses require you to pass external certification exams (CompTIA, Cisco, AWS, etc.). These use different proctoring:

  • Pearson VUE: CompTIA, Cisco, AWS, Microsoft certifications
  • ISC2: SSCP, CCSP, CISSP
  • PeopleCert: ITIL certifications

Each provider has their own proctoring requirements—always read the specific instructions when scheduling external exams. For certification prep strategies, see our WGU Certification Guide.

Tips for Exam Day Success

Physical Preparation

  • Good night's sleep: Avoid late-night cramming before the exam
  • Eat beforehand: Have a proper meal—you can't eat during the exam
  • Use the bathroom: No breaks allowed once the exam starts
  • Dress appropriately: You're on camera (no hats or sunglasses)

Mental Preparation

  • Review the coaching report: Know your weak areas from the pre-assessment
  • Arrive early: Rushing increases anxiety
  • Don't over-study the day of: Trust your preparation
  • Stay calm: If you hit a hard question, move on and return to it

Technical Preparation

  • Restart computer: Clear any background processes
  • Close all apps: Guardian will prompt anyway, but do it beforehand
  • Check internet: Run a speed test
  • Test webcam and mic: Quick verification before connecting to proctor
