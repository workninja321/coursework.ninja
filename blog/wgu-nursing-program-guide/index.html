WGU Nursing Programs: RN to BSN and MSN Pathways Explained

December 17, 2025 11 min read Degree Guides

WGU offers some of the most flexible and affordable nursing programs for working RNs looking to advance their careers. Whether you're pursuing your BSN, MSN, or even nurse practitioner credentials, WGU's competency-based model lets you leverage your existing knowledge and experience to graduate faster.

This guide breaks down every WGU nursing pathway, comparing costs, clinical requirements, completion times, and what real students say about their experiences.

RN to BSN Program

The RN to BSN is WGU's most popular nursing program, designed for working RNs who need their bachelor's degree for career advancement, management roles, or graduate school admission.

Program Highlights

  • 23 courses total (many transfer from your ADN)
  • Only 35 clinical hours required (can often be completed at your workplace)
  • No homework, discussion forums, or group projects
  • Pass/fail grading - demonstrate competency and move on
  • Transfer up to 90 credits from prior education
Fast Completers: Many motivated RNs finish in one 6-month term ($5,325 total). One student reported completing it in 3 months. The key is that you get credit for what you already know as a working nurse.

Course Examples

Many courses align with what you do daily as an RN:

  • Health Assessment: "Can be done in a couple days. You do a simulation on an avatar. We do this EVERY DAY as nurses!"
  • Care of the Older Adult: "Completed in one afternoon. Review notes and take the test."
  • Community Health: Requires a community project but is manageable
  • Leadership and Management: Builds on your clinical experience

MSN Programs

WGU offers several MSN specializations for nurses ready to advance into leadership, education, or advanced practice roles.

Non-NP MSN Tracks

  • Nursing Education: Prepare to teach future nurses
  • Nursing Leadership and Management: Administrative and executive roles
  • Nursing Informatics: Bridge healthcare and technology

These tracks cost $5,035/term, require approximately 200 field experience hours, and 62% of graduates finish within 24 months.

Nurse Practitioner Tracks

  • Family Nurse Practitioner (FNP): Primary care for patients of all ages
  • Psychiatric Mental Health NP (PMHNP): Mental health specialty
State Restrictions: FNP is NOT available in California, District of Columbia, Louisiana, New York, North Dakota, or Washington due to clinical placement requirements. All clinical rotations must be completed in your home state.

NP programs cost $6,955/term and take approximately 2.5 years including 500+ clinical hours.

Admission Requirements

RN to BSN Requirements

  • Active, unencumbered RN license
  • Associate degree in nursing (ADN) or diploma from accredited program
  • No minimum GPA requirement
  • No RN work experience required

MSN Requirements

  • Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) from accredited institution
  • Active, unencumbered RN license
  • For NP programs: residence in an approved state
  • Criminal background check before clinical placement
  • Current immunization records

Acceleration Strategies

WGU's competency-based model is perfect for experienced nurses. Here's how to finish faster:

  1. Transfer maximum credits: Your ADN coursework transfers. Some students start with only 10-12 courses remaining.
  2. Take assessments immediately: If you know the material, schedule the exam right away.
  3. Leverage your experience: Much of nursing school teaches what you already do daily as a working RN.
  4. Complete clinicals at work: The 35 BSN clinical hours can often be done during your regular shifts with preceptor approval.
  5. Study during downtime: Night shift nurses often study during slow periods.
Student Experience: "I had no life outside of work and school for 6 months, but it was worth it. No homework, no group projects, no discussion forums. If you know the info, just take the proctored exam or write the paper and you're done."

GPA Considerations

Since WGU uses pass/fail grading, your GPA will be 3.0. This is a common concern for students planning to pursue NP or DNP programs.

The good news: Many graduate programs accept WGU degrees. One graduate shared: "I was accepted to three different DNP programs with my WGU MSN. The GPA thing is rapidly becoming a non-issue."

Schools increasingly understand competency-based education and focus on your nursing experience, certifications, and interview performance rather than GPA alone.

Career Advancement

A BSN or MSN opens doors to:

  • Management roles: Charge nurse, nurse manager, director of nursing
  • Specialized units: Many hospitals require BSN for ICU, OR, and specialty units
  • Education: Clinical instructor, nursing faculty positions
  • Informatics: Healthcare IT and EHR implementation roles
  • Advanced Practice: NP, CNS, CRNA (with additional education)

Many hospitals now require BSN within 3-5 years of hire, making this investment increasingly necessary for career stability.
