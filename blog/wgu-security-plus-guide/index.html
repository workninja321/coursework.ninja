WGU Security+ Certification: Complete Study and Exam Guide

January 2, 2025 12 min read Exam Preparation

CompTIA Security+ is one of the most valuable certifications in the WGU IT and Cybersecurity programs. It validates your foundational cybersecurity knowledge and is recognized globally by employers. At WGU, passing the Security+ exam (currently SY0-701) completes the associated course, so your exam prep IS your course completion strategy.

This guide covers the SY0-701 exam objectives, study strategies, resources, and practical tips to pass on your first attempt.

Exam overview

The CompTIA Security+ SY0-701 exam launched in November 2023 and includes updated content reflecting modern security practices:

  • Exam code: SY0-701
  • Number of questions: Up to 90
  • Question types: Multiple choice and performance-based questions (PBQs)
  • Time limit: 90 minutes
  • Passing score: 750 out of 900
  • Prerequisite: None required, but Network+ recommended

Exam domains and weightings

The SY0-701 exam covers five domains. Understanding the weighting helps prioritize your study time:

Domain Weight Focus Areas
1.0 General Security Concepts 12% Security controls, CIA triad, zero trust, cryptography basics
2.0 Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations 22% Threat actors, attack types, indicators of compromise, mitigation
3.0 Security Architecture 18% Network security, cloud, virtualization, secure infrastructure
4.0 Security Operations 28% Monitoring, incident response, vulnerability management, automation
5.0 Security Program Management and Oversight 20% Governance, risk, compliance, policies, awareness training
Pro tip: Domain 4 (Security Operations) is 28% of the exam. Prioritize this section in your studies, especially incident response and monitoring concepts.

What's new in SY0-701

If you studied for the older SY0-601, here are key changes:

  • Zero Trust: Significantly expanded coverage of zero trust architecture
  • Cloud security: More emphasis on cloud-specific security controls
  • Automation: New focus on security automation and orchestration
  • Governance: Increased coverage of compliance and risk management
  • Streamlined domains: Reduced from 6 to 5 domains with reorganized content

Key concepts to master

General security concepts (Domain 1)

  • CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
  • AAA: Authentication, Authorization, Accounting
  • Security controls: Preventive, detective, corrective, deterrent, compensating
  • Zero Trust: Never trust, always verify; least privilege; microsegmentation
  • Encryption types: Symmetric vs asymmetric, hashing, digital signatures

Threats and attacks (Domain 2)

  • Threat actors: Nation-states, hacktivists, insiders, script kiddies, APTs
  • Social engineering: Phishing, vishing, smishing, pretexting, tailgating
  • Malware types: Ransomware, trojans, rootkits, fileless malware
  • Network attacks: DoS/DDoS, man-in-the-middle, DNS attacks, ARP poisoning
  • Application attacks: SQL injection, XSS, CSRF, buffer overflow

Security architecture (Domain 3)

  • Network security: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs, network segmentation
  • Cloud models: IaaS, PaaS, SaaS; public, private, hybrid
  • Secure protocols: TLS, SSH, IPSec, SFTP, HTTPS
  • Identity and access: MFA, SSO, federation, LDAP, SAML, OAuth

Security operations (Domain 4)

  • Monitoring: SIEM, log analysis, packet capture
  • Incident response: Preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, lessons learned
  • Vulnerability management: Scanning, assessment, prioritization, remediation
  • Digital forensics: Chain of custody, order of volatility, evidence handling

Governance (Domain 5)

  • Frameworks: NIST, ISO 27001, CIS Controls, COBIT
  • Compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOX
  • Risk management: Risk assessment, risk response, business impact analysis
  • Policies: AUP, password policies, data classification, retention policies

Performance-based questions (PBQs)

PBQs are simulation-style questions that test practical skills. Common PBQ scenarios include:

  • Log analysis: Identify attacks from log entries
  • Firewall rules: Configure ACLs or firewall policies
  • Network diagrams: Identify security issues or proper placement of devices
  • Command-line tools: Use nmap, netstat, or similar tools
  • Certificate management: Configure PKI or certificate settings
PBQ strategy: Skip PBQs initially and return to them after completing multiple-choice questions. They're time-consuming and you don't want to rush the easier questions.

Recommended study resources

Primary resources

  • Professor Messer (YouTube): Free video course covering all objectives - essential watching
  • CompTIA CertMaster: Official study materials (included with WGU)
  • Jason Dion practice exams: Highly recommended for realistic practice questions

Supplementary resources

  • CompTIA Security+ Get Certified Get Ahead (Darril Gibson): Excellent study guide
  • ExamCompass: Free practice questions by objective
  • CompTIA Labs: Hands-on practice for PBQ preparation

6-week study plan

Week 1: General Security Concepts (Domain 1)

  • Watch Professor Messer videos for Domain 1
  • Create flashcards for acronyms and definitions
  • Focus on: CIA triad, security controls, cryptography basics

Week 2: Threats and Attacks (Domain 2 - Part 1)

  • Study threat actors and attack vectors
  • Learn social engineering techniques
  • Create a mind map of malware types

Week 3: Threats and Mitigations (Domain 2 - Part 2)

  • Network and application attacks
  • Vulnerability types and mitigation strategies
  • Take first practice exam to assess gaps

Week 4: Security Architecture (Domain 3)

  • Network security devices and their placement
  • Cloud security concepts
  • Identity and access management

Week 5: Security Operations (Domain 4)

  • Incident response lifecycle (memorize the phases)
  • Monitoring and detection tools
  • Vulnerability management process

Week 6: Governance and Review (Domain 5 + Final Prep)

  • Frameworks and compliance requirements
  • Risk management concepts
  • Take 2-3 full practice exams
  • Review weak areas identified in practice tests

Exam day tips

  1. Skip PBQs first: Complete multiple-choice questions first, then return to PBQs
  2. Flag uncertain answers: Mark questions you're unsure about for review
  3. Read carefully: Look for keywords like "BEST," "MOST," "FIRST" - they change the answer
  4. Eliminate wrong answers: Narrow down to 2 options, then make your best choice
  5. Watch the clock: Aim for 1 minute per question average to leave time for review
  6. Don't second-guess: Your first instinct is often correct unless you find clear evidence otherwise

Common pitfalls to avoid

  • Only reading without practice: You must take practice exams to pass
  • Ignoring PBQ practice: These can make or break your score
  • Memorizing without understanding: The exam tests application, not just recall
  • Skipping acronyms: Know what every acronym stands for AND what it does
  • Underestimating governance: Domain 5 is 20% of the exam - don't neglect it

Achieving the passing score

The 750/900 passing score means you need roughly 83% correct. However, questions are weighted differently, so:

  • Aim for 85%+ on practice exams before scheduling the real exam
  • Be strong in all domains - you can't pass by mastering just one or two
  • PBQs are worth more, so practice them extensively

After passing

Once you pass Security+:

  • The course completion at WGU is automatic
  • The certification is valid for 3 years
  • Maintain it through Continuing Education Units (CEUs)
  • Consider pursuing CySA+ or PenTest+ as your next certification
