WGU Accelerated BS to MS Programs: Earn Two Degrees Faster

January 2, 2026 10 min read Degree Guides

Most students assume earning a bachelor's and master's degree requires completing two entirely separate programs. WGU's accelerated BS to MS programs challenge that assumption by integrating graduate-level coursework into your bachelor's degree, letting you earn both credentials with fewer total courses and less time.

If you know you want a master's degree eventually, the accelerated track is one of the smartest moves you can make. This guide covers which programs offer the accelerated option, how much time and money you can save, and whether the accelerated path is right for you.

Available Accelerated Programs

WGU currently offers accelerated bachelor's to master's programs in three high-demand technology fields:

1. Information Technology (BSIT to MSITM)

The IT accelerated program combines the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology with the Master of Science in Information Technology Management. This path prepares you for technical leadership roles that bridge IT operations and business strategy.

  • Industry certifications included (networking, security, project management)
  • Skills: scripting, networking, systems administration, data management, IT governance
  • Career paths: IT Manager, Systems Administrator, IT Director, CIO track

2. Software Engineering (BS to MS)

The Software Engineering accelerated program takes you from foundational programming through advanced software architecture and AI concepts. The master's portion focuses on emerging technologies and technical leadership.

  • Covers: programming, web development, mobile apps, UX design, software QA
  • Graduate focus: artificial intelligence, advanced architecture, technical leadership
  • Career paths: Software Engineer, Senior Developer, Technical Lead, Engineering Manager

3. Computer Science (BS to MS)

The Computer Science accelerated program combines ABET-accredited bachelor's coursework with graduate studies in applied reasoning, AI foundations, and advanced computing concepts.

  • Strong mathematical and theoretical foundation
  • Graduate coursework in Unix/Linux, computer architecture, artificial intelligence
  • Career paths: Software Developer, Data Scientist, Machine Learning Engineer, Research positions
Tip: The BSIT to MSITM track is the most popular accelerated option because it combines technical depth with management skills, opening doors to both technical and leadership roles.

How the Accelerated Structure Works

The accelerated programs are not simply "do both degrees." They are specifically designed to reduce redundancy by integrating graduate coursework into your bachelor's program.

Bridge Courses

After completing the core bachelor's requirements, you take "bridge courses" that count toward both degrees. These courses are graduate-level but taken during your bachelor's phase, reducing the total courses needed for the master's.

Streamlined Progression

  1. Phase 1: Complete bachelor's core courses and general education
  2. Phase 2: Take bridge courses (count for both degrees)
  3. Phase 3: Receive your bachelor's degree
  4. Phase 4: Complete remaining master's courses
  5. Phase 5: Receive your master's degree

You officially receive your bachelor's degree before starting the master's-only portion, so you can update your resume and job applications immediately after completing undergraduate requirements.

How Much Time and Money You Save

The exact savings depend on your transfer credits and pace, but the accelerated structure typically reduces total requirements by 15-25% compared to completing both degrees separately.

Estimated Savings

  • Course reduction: 8-15 fewer courses compared to separate programs
  • Time savings: 6-12 months for motivated students
  • Tuition savings: One or more terms at approximately $4,000 each
  • Certification value: Industry certs included save $300-500+ in exam fees
Important: These savings assume you complete the accelerated program as designed. If you switch out of the accelerated track midway, some bridge course credits may not transfer optimally to a standalone master's program.

Who Should Consider the Accelerated Track

The accelerated programs are ideal for specific types of students:

Good Candidates

  • Career-focused students: You know you want to move into management or senior technical roles
  • Accelerators: You have strong time management and can maintain momentum through graduate coursework
  • Employer-supported students: Your employer offers tuition reimbursement and you want to maximize the benefit
  • Long-term planners: You are committed to completing both degrees without significant breaks

Consider Alternatives If

  • You are unsure whether you want a master's degree
  • You may need to pause your education for extended periods
  • You want flexibility to switch to a different master's field later
  • You prefer to gain work experience between degrees

Admission and Enrollment

Enrolling in the accelerated track requires meeting the standard WGU admission requirements plus demonstrating readiness for an intensive program.

Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Standard WGU admission requirements for the chosen field
  • Commitment to completing both degrees in sequence
  • Discussion with enrollment counselor about accelerated track fit

Switching to Accelerated

If you are already enrolled in a standard bachelor's program at WGU, you may be able to switch to the accelerated track. Contact your enrollment counselor as early as possible to discuss the transition. The earlier you switch, the more benefit you receive from the integrated structure.

Strategies for Success

Completing an accelerated program requires sustained effort. These strategies help you maintain momentum:

  1. Maximize transfer credits: Use Sophia Learning or Study.com before enrollment to knock out general education courses
  2. Plan your terms: Map out your entire program with your mentor to identify the most efficient course sequence
  3. Build momentum early: Complete easier courses first to build confidence before tackling bridge courses
  4. Leverage certifications: The included certifications have career value, invest real study time in them
  5. Communicate with your mentor: Regular check-ins keep you accountable and help identify problems early

For more acceleration strategies, see our WGU Acceleration Guide and Transfer Credits Guide.
