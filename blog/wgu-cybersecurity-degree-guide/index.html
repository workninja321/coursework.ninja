WGU Cybersecurity Degree Guide: BSCSIA Program, Certifications & Career Paths

December 17, 2025 14 min read Degree Programs

WGU's Bachelor of Science in Cybersecurity & Information Assurance (BSCSIA) is one of the most certification-rich cybersecurity programs available. With up to 16 industry certifications built into the curriculum and ABET accreditation, it's designed to make graduates job-ready from day one.

This guide covers everything you need to know about the program: certifications included, coursework structure, career outcomes, and strategies to complete faster.

Program Overview

The BSCSIA program prepares students for cybersecurity roles through a combination of academic coursework and industry certification preparation. Unlike traditional programs that focus solely on theory, WGU integrates real-world certifications into every aspect of the curriculum.

Program Highlights

  • 122 Competency Units (CUs): Equivalent to approximately 120 credit hours
  • ABET Accredited: Gold standard for computing programs
  • NSA/DHS CAE-CD Designated: Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense
  • Flat-Rate Tuition: ~$4,400 per 6-month term
  • No Hidden Fees: Certification exam costs included

Prerequisites

To enroll in the BSCSIA program, you need:

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Basic IT knowledge (recommended but not required)
  • No prior certifications required—you'll earn them in the program

Curriculum Structure

The 122 CUs are divided across general education, IT fundamentals, and cybersecurity specialization courses.

General Education (~30 CUs)

  • English Composition
  • Applied Probability and Statistics
  • Critical Thinking and Logic
  • Introduction to Communication
  • Natural Science courses
  • Humanities elective

Many general education requirements can be satisfied through transfer credits from Sophia Learning or Study.com, potentially saving significant time and money.

IT Fundamentals (~35 CUs)

  • IT Foundations (CompTIA A+ prep)
  • Network and Security Foundations (Network+ prep)
  • Linux Foundations (LPI Linux Essentials)
  • Data Management (SQL fundamentals)
  • Web Development Foundations
  • IT Leadership Foundations (ITIL)

Cybersecurity Core (~57 CUs)

  • Security Foundations (Security+ prep)
  • Security Operations (CySA+ prep)
  • Penetration Testing (PenTest+ prep)
  • Secure Systems Design
  • Cloud Security (CCSP prep)
  • Digital Forensics
  • Ethical Hacking
  • Security Architecture
  • Cybersecurity Capstone

Course Difficulty & Order

Understanding which courses are challenging helps you plan your terms strategically. For exam-taking strategies, see our OA Exam Tips guide.

Easier Courses (Good for Building Momentum)

  • IT Foundations: CompTIA A+ content, manageable if you have any IT background
  • Linux Foundations: LPI Linux Essentials, straightforward with practice
  • Web Development Foundations: Basic HTML/CSS, quick completion
  • IT Leadership: ITIL Foundation, mostly memorization

Moderate Courses

  • Network and Security Foundations: Network+ content, requires solid study
  • Data Management: SQL and database concepts
  • Security Foundations: Security+ is substantial but achievable

Challenging Courses (Plan Extra Time)

  • CySA+: Deep analytical content, typically 4-6 weeks
  • PenTest+: Hands-on penetration testing skills required
  • SSCP: Broad security knowledge, dense material
  • CCSP: Cloud security is complex, often the hardest cert course
  • Capstone: Major project requiring real-world application
Course Order Matters: Work with your Program Mentor to sequence courses properly. Foundation courses (A+, Network+) should come before advanced security courses (CySA+, PenTest+).

Acceleration Strategies

The BSCSIA program can be completed in 2-3 years at a moderate pace, but dedicated students finish in 12-18 months. Here's how to accelerate:

Before Enrollment

  1. Maximize transfer credits: Complete Sophia Learning or Study.com courses for general education
  2. Get CompTIA certs early: A+ and Network+ can transfer as course credit
  3. Review the degree plan: Understand exactly what courses you'll face

During the Program

  • Front-load easy courses: Build momentum with Web Dev, ITIL, Linux Essentials
  • Batch certification courses: Study related certs together (A+ → Network+ → Security+)
  • Use multiple resources: Don't rely solely on WGU materials—use Udemy, Professor Messer, etc.
  • Take pre-assessments immediately: Know your gaps before studying

For comprehensive acceleration strategies, see our WGU Acceleration Guide and Study Tips for Working Adults.
