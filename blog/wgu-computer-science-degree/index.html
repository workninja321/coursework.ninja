WGU Computer Science Degree Guide 2025: BSCS Complete Breakdown

Updated January 2025 | 15 min read

WGU's Bachelor of Science in Computer Science is the most rigorous IT program the university offers. Unlike the BSIT which focuses on practical skills and certifications, the BSCS emphasizes theoretical foundations - algorithms, data structures, discrete math, and software engineering principles.

This guide breaks down everything you need to know: curriculum structure, the math courses everyone worries about, transfer credit strategies, and realistic career outcomes.

Program Snapshot: 121 Competency Units | ~39 Courses | $3,755/term | No certifications included | Strong math foundation required

BSCS vs BSIT: Which Should You Choose?

This is the most common question prospective students ask. Here's the honest breakdown:

Choose BSCS If You Want:

  • Software engineering roles at FAANG companies
  • Graduate school (MS/PhD in CS)
  • Algorithm-heavy positions
  • Deep understanding of computer theory
  • To pass technical coding interviews

Choose BSIT If You Want:

  • System administration / DevOps roles
  • IT management positions
  • Industry certifications (CompTIA, AWS, etc.)
  • Faster path to graduation
  • Less math-intensive curriculum

Also consider the Cybersecurity degree (BSCSIA) if security is your focus - it includes 16 industry certifications.

Reality Check: Many successful software developers have BSIT degrees. The BSCS is advantageous for competitive tech roles but not strictly required. Choose based on your career goals and math comfort level.

The 5 Hardest Courses (And How to Pass Them)

1. Discrete Mathematics I & II

Why it's hard: Mathematical proofs, abstract thinking, completely different from any math you've done before.

Strategy: Use TrevTutor on YouTube (free), work through the Zybooks material twice, practice proofs daily. Budget 3-4 weeks per course.

2. Data Structures and Algorithms I & II

Why it's hard: Requires both coding skills AND theoretical understanding. Time complexity analysis trips up many students.

Strategy: Use NeetCode or LeetCode to practice. Implement every data structure from scratch. Understand Big O notation cold. These courses directly prep you for technical interviews.

3. Software II - Advanced Java (C195)

Why it's hard: Large PA project combining JavaFX, MySQL, JDBC, and business logic. Many moving parts.

Strategy: Start the PA immediately, don't spend weeks on course material first. Watch YouTube tutorials for JavaFX. Budget 3-4 weeks minimum.

4. Calculus I

Why it's hard: If your last math class was years ago, the learning curve is steep.

Strategy: Transfer it from Sophia or Study.com. If you must take it at WGU, use Professor Leonard's YouTube series (free, excellent).

5. Operating Systems for Programmers

Why it's hard: Low-level concepts (memory management, process scheduling) are abstract and unfamiliar.

Strategy: Use the course material plus "Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces" (free online textbook). Focus on understanding concepts, not memorization.

Can You Accelerate the BSCS?

Yes, but it's harder than accelerating other WGU programs. Here's realistic timelines:

Scenario Realistic Timeline Courses/Term
No transfer credits, working full-time 3-4 years 3-4 courses/term
Max transfer credits, working full-time 2-2.5 years 4-5 courses/term
Max transfers, full-time student 1-1.5 years 6-8 courses/term
Strong CS background, full-time student 6-12 months 8+ courses/term

Acceleration Strategy

  1. Transfer everything possible - Gen ed from Sophia, Calculus from Study.com
  2. Front-load easy courses - Build momentum with quick wins first
  3. Parallel track - Work on PA while studying for next OA

FAANG Reality: Yes, WGU grads do get hired at Google, Amazon, Microsoft, etc. But you need to supplement your degree with: LeetCode practice, personal projects, and strong interview prep. The degree opens doors; your skills close deals.

The Capstone Project (C964)

Your capstone is a significant project demonstrating your CS skills. Common project types:

  • Machine Learning application - Classification, prediction, or NLP project
  • Full-stack web application - Complete app with database, API, frontend
  • Mobile application - Android or cross-platform app
  • Data analysis project - Large dataset analysis with visualization

Capstone Tips

  1. Start thinking about your topic 2-3 courses before you reach it
  2. Choose something you're genuinely interested in
  3. Scope appropriately - it should take 4-6 weeks, not 4-6 months
  4. Document everything as you build
  5. See our capstone guide for detailed strategies

Frequently Asked Questions

Is WGU's Computer Science degree ABET accredited?

No, WGU's BSCS is not ABET accredited. However, WGU is regionally accredited by NWCCU, which is recognized by employers and graduate schools. ABET accreditation is more relevant for engineering programs and is rarely required for software development careers.

How hard is the math in WGU's Computer Science program?

WGU's BSCS requires Calculus I, Discrete Mathematics I & II, and Linear Algebra. The math is rigorous but manageable with proper preparation. Many students use transfer credits from Sophia or Study.com for Calculus to reduce stress. Discrete Math is unavoidable and essential - budget extra time for these courses.

What programming languages does WGU CS teach?

WGU's Computer Science program primarily uses Python and Java. You'll also work with SQL for databases, and gain exposure to web technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) in certain courses. The focus is on concepts that transfer across languages rather than language-specific skills.

Can I get into graduate school with a WGU CS degree?

Yes, WGU graduates have been accepted to graduate programs at Georgia Tech (OMSCS), UT Austin, and other respected programs. Strong GPA, GRE scores, and relevant work experience help. The regional accreditation is what matters for graduate school admission.

Is the BSCS worth it over the BSIT?

It depends on your goals. BSCS is better for software engineering roles, graduate school, and algorithm-heavy positions. BSIT is better for IT management, system administration, and includes industry certifications. Both lead to good careers in tech.
