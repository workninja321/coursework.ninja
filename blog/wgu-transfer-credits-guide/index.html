WGU Transfer Credits Guide: Maximize Credits from Sophia, Study.com & More

December 17, 2025 12 min read Degree Planning

Transfer credits are the single most powerful tool for accelerating through your WGU degree. Every credit you transfer is one less you have to earn at WGU—and since WGU charges flat tuition per term, more transfer credits means fewer terms and thousands saved.

WGU accepts up to 75% of your degree requirements as transfer credits. For a typical 120-credit bachelor's degree, that's up to 90 credits you could bring in before even starting. This guide covers every major transfer credit source and how to maximize your credits.

Why Transfer Credits Matter

Understanding WGU's pricing model explains why transfer credits are so valuable:

  • Flat tuition per term: You pay around $4,000-$4,500 per 6-month term regardless of credits completed
  • Each transfer credit reduces your workload: Fewer courses to complete means faster graduation
  • External credits are often cheaper: Sophia costs $99/month vs. $4,000+ per WGU term
  • No limit on courses per term: But starting with fewer courses makes acceleration easier

For example: Transferring 30 credits from Sophia Learning (2-3 months at $99/month = ~$300) could save you an entire WGU term worth $4,500+. That's a 15x return on investment.

Sophia Learning: Best Value for Transfer Credits

Sophia Learning is the go-to transfer credit source for WGU students. Here's why:

Sophia Basics

  • Cost: $99/month for unlimited courses (or $79/month annual plan)
  • Format: Self-paced online courses with quizzes and touchstones
  • Speed: Most students complete 3-5 courses per month
  • Credits: ACE-recommended credits that transfer directly to WGU

WGU-Aligned Sophia Courses

Sophia offers 27+ courses that align directly with WGU requirements. Popular options include:

  • Business: Accounting, Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, Business Law, Marketing
  • IT: Introduction to IT, Project Management, Web Development
  • General Education: English Composition, Communications, Psychology, Statistics
  • Healthcare: Medical Terminology, Anatomy & Physiology
Pro Tip: Before subscribing to Sophia, request an unofficial transcript evaluation from your WGU enrollment counselor. They'll tell you exactly which Sophia courses will transfer to your specific program.

Sophia Completion Strategy

  1. Sign up for a free Sophia account and review the course list
  2. Map Sophia courses to your WGU program requirements
  3. Start with your strongest subjects to build momentum
  4. Complete all quizzes first, then focus on Touchstones (written assignments)
  5. Aim for 4-5 courses per month to maximize your subscription
  6. Request transcripts 2-3 weeks before your WGU term starts

Study.com: More Course Options

Study.com offers a broader course selection than Sophia, though at a higher price point.

Study.com Basics

  • Cost: $199.99/month (College Accelerator plan)
  • Format: Video lessons followed by quizzes and proctored final exams
  • Speed: 2-4 courses per month typical
  • Credits: ACE-recommended, transfers as lower-division credit

When to Choose Study.com Over Sophia

  • You need courses not offered on Sophia
  • You prefer video-based learning over reading
  • Your program requires specific courses only Study.com offers
  • You've exhausted all relevant Sophia courses
Note: Study.com requires proctored final exams ($70 each). Factor this into your total cost calculations. Some students complete all coursework, then schedule all exams in one month to minimize subscription costs.

Straighterline: College-Level Courses

Straighterline offers fully accredited college courses that transfer to WGU and many other institutions.

Straighterline Basics

  • Cost: $99/month membership + $59-$79 per course
  • Format: Traditional online courses with assignments and proctored exams
  • Speed: 1-2 courses per month typical
  • Credits: ACE-recommended college credits

Best Straighterline Courses for WGU

  • College Algebra (if you struggle with math)
  • English Composition I and II
  • Introduction to Business
  • Principles of Management
  • Anatomy & Physiology (for nursing students)

CLEP and DSST Exams

If you already have knowledge in a subject area, CLEP and DSST exams let you prove competency through testing.

CLEP Exams

  • Cost: $93 per exam (plus testing center fee)
  • Format: 90-minute computer-based exam at a testing center
  • Subjects: 34 exams covering business, sciences, humanities, and more
  • Preparation: Use Modern States (free) or InstantCert for study materials

DSST Exams

  • Cost: $100 per exam
  • Format: Similar to CLEP, computer-based at testing centers
  • Subjects: 30+ exams including technical and specialized topics
Free Prep: Modern States offers free CLEP preparation courses. Complete a course and they'll give you a voucher for a free CLEP exam—including the testing fee. This makes CLEP essentially free.

Military Training and Credits

WGU is military-friendly and accepts credits from military training and education:

  • Joint Services Transcript (JST): Army, Marine Corps, Navy, Coast Guard training
  • Community College of the Air Force (CCAF): Air Force education credits
  • DANTES/DSST: Military-sponsored testing programs
  • ACE Military Evaluations: Formal credit recommendations for military training

Request your JST or CCAF transcript early—processing can take several weeks.

Transfer Credit Timeline

Timing is critical for transfer credits. Here's the optimal timeline:

Before Applying to WGU

  1. 3-6 months before: Start Sophia/Study.com courses
  2. 2-3 months before: Complete external courses and request transcripts
  3. 1-2 months before: Submit WGU application and send transcripts
  4. 2-4 weeks before term: Confirm all credits have been evaluated
Critical: Transfer credits must be submitted and evaluated BEFORE your term starts to count. Credits submitted after your term begins won't be applied until your next term—potentially costing you a full term's tuition.

After Enrollment

You can still transfer credits between terms, but it requires careful planning:

  • Complete external courses during your current WGU term
  • Request transcripts immediately upon completion
  • Work with your Program Mentor to apply credits before next term
  • Be aware: you can't drop a WGU course you've already started

Strategy: Maximize Your Transfer Credits

Here's a step-by-step strategy to maximize transfer credits before starting WGU:

  1. Get your program requirements: Request the degree plan from WGU admissions
  2. Request unofficial evaluation: Have WGU evaluate any existing credits
  3. Map remaining requirements: Identify which can be satisfied externally
  4. Prioritize by cost efficiency: Sophia > CLEP > Study.com > Straighterline
  5. Create a completion timeline: Work backwards from your desired start date
  6. Execute systematically: Complete courses, request transcripts, verify transfer

Common Transfer Credit Mistakes

Avoid these costly mistakes:

  • Waiting too long to request transcripts: Processing takes 2-4 weeks
  • Not verifying transfer before enrolling: Get written confirmation
  • Taking courses that don't transfer: Always verify alignment first
  • Exceeding the 75% limit: Extra credits beyond 75% won't count
  • Missing the term deadline: Late credits wait until next term
  • Not considering certification credits: Existing certs may transfer
