WGU MBA Program: Complete Guide to Fast-Track Your Business Degree

Published: December 17, 2025 • 12 min read • Category: Degree Guides

The Western Governors University MBA is one of the most affordable and flexible graduate business degrees available. With WGU's competency-based model, motivated students can complete their MBA in as little as 6-12 months, saving thousands compared to traditional programs.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about the WGU MBA: program structure, courses, costs, acceleration strategies, and tips from successful graduates.

WGU MBA Program Overview

The WGU Master of Business Administration is a 36 competency unit (CU) program designed for working professionals. Unlike traditional MBA programs with fixed schedules, WGU allows you to move through courses at your own pace.

Key Program Features

  • Competency-Based: Advance by demonstrating mastery, not seat time
  • Flat-Rate Tuition: Take as many courses as you can per 6-month term
  • No GMAT Required: Admission based on bachelor's degree and interview
  • ACBSP Accredited: Recognized business program accreditation
  • 100% Online: Study anytime, anywhere
  • Mentor Support: Dedicated program mentor throughout your journey

WGU MBA at a Glance

Total CUs: 36 Competency Units (11 courses)

Average Completion: 12-18 months

Fastest Completion: 6 months (one term)

Cost per Term: ~$4,530

Total Cost (average): $9,000-$13,500

Understanding the Assessment Types

The WGU MBA uses two types of assessments:

  • Objective Assessments (OAs): Multiple-choice proctored exams testing your knowledge of concepts. Six courses use OAs. These can often be completed quickly if you have business experience.
  • Performance Assessments (PAs): Written papers and projects demonstrating your ability to apply concepts. Five courses use PAs. These require more time but allow you to show depth of understanding.

How to Accelerate Through the WGU MBA

Completing the WGU MBA in one term (6 months) is absolutely achievable with the right strategy. Here's how successful accelerators approach the program:

Before You Start

  • Check for transfer credits: Prior graduate courses may transfer
  • Leverage your experience: Business professionals often move faster
  • Clear your schedule: Plan for 20-30 hours/week of study time
  • Set a term goal: Decide how many courses you want to complete

Recommended Course Order

Week 1-2: Start with Your Strengths

Begin with OA courses in areas where you have experience. Many students knock out C207 (Data-Driven Decision Making) and C218 (Marketing) quickly if they have background in these areas.

Week 3-6: Tackle PA Courses

Move to PA courses like C200 (Managing Organizations) and C202 (Human Capital). These require written papers but can be completed efficiently with good time management.

Week 7-10: Challenging Content

Address more challenging courses like C214 (Financial Management) and C213 (Accounting). Give yourself extra time if these subjects are new to you.

Week 11-16: Clean Up and Capstone

Complete remaining courses and dedicate the final weeks to C216 (MBA Capstone). The capstone requires creating a comprehensive business analysis and recommendations.

Important Note

WGU restricts you to one active course at a time initially. Once you demonstrate competency, your mentor can approve adding additional courses. Build trust early by completing courses efficiently.

WGU MBA Course Tips

Easiest Courses (Start Here)

C207 - Data-Driven Decision Making: If you have any experience with data analysis or statistics, this OA can be completed quickly. Focus on understanding basic statistical concepts and Excel formulas.

C218 - MBA Marketing: Marketing fundamentals that most business professionals already understand. The OA covers 4Ps, market segmentation, and positioning strategies.

C204 - Management Communication: A PA course focused on professional communication. If you write well and have business experience, this paper writes itself.

Most Challenging Courses

C214 - Financial Management: The most challenging OA for many students. Requires understanding time value of money, capital budgeting, and financial statement analysis. Dedicate extra study time here.

C213 - Accounting for Decision Makers: Challenging if you don't have an accounting background. Focus on understanding financial statements, ratios, and managerial accounting concepts.

C216 - MBA Capstone: The final course requiring a comprehensive business proposal. Give yourself at least 2-3 weeks for this one.

Admission Requirements

Getting into the WGU MBA is straightforward:

Basic Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree: From a regionally accredited institution
  • Minimum GPA: No minimum GPA requirement
  • GMAT/GRE: Not required
  • Work experience: Not required (but helpful for acceleration)

Application Process

  1. Submit online application
  2. Provide official transcripts
  3. Complete admissions interview
  4. Receive acceptance decision
  5. Choose your start date (1st of each month)

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the WGU MBA take to complete?

The WGU MBA can be completed in as little as 6 months if you can dedicate significant time to studying. On average, most students complete the program in 12-18 months. WGU's competency-based model allows you to accelerate through courses where you already have knowledge and experience.

How much does the WGU MBA cost?

The WGU MBA costs approximately $4,530 per 6-month term (as of 2025). Since you can take as many courses as you can complete each term, the total cost ranges from $4,530 (one term) to around $18,000+ depending on your pace. Most students complete it in 2-3 terms ($9,000-$13,500).

Is the WGU MBA respected by employers?

Yes, the WGU MBA is accredited by ACBSP (Accreditation Council for Business Schools and Programs) and WGU is regionally accredited by NWCCU. Major employers including Amazon, Google, and Microsoft have partnerships with WGU. Your skills and experience matter more to employers than the specific school.

What are the admission requirements for the WGU MBA?

WGU MBA admission requires a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. There is no GMAT or GRE requirement. You'll also need to complete a brief admissions interview. Professional experience is not required but is beneficial for accelerating through the program.

Final Thoughts

The WGU MBA represents one of the best values in graduate business education. With no GMAT requirements, flexible scheduling, and the ability to accelerate, it's an excellent choice for motivated professionals who want to advance their careers without breaking the bank.

Whether you complete it in 6 months or 2 years, a WGU MBA gives you the credentials and knowledge to take your career to the next level.
