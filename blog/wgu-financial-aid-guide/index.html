WGU Financial Aid Guide 2025: Scholarships, Grants & Loans

Updated January 2025 | 12 min read

One of the biggest advantages of WGU is its affordability compared to traditional universities. But "affordable" doesn't mean "free" - and smart students take full advantage of financial aid to reduce their out-of-pocket costs even further.

This guide covers every financial aid option available to WGU students in 2025, from federal grants and loans to scholarships and employer tuition assistance. Whether you're starting fresh or transferring credits to accelerate your degree, understanding your financial aid options can save you thousands.

Key Stat: WGU awarded over $19 million in scholarships in 2024, with the average scholarship recipient receiving $2,500-$4,000 toward their degree.

Unlike traditional universities that charge per credit hour, WGU charges a flat rate per 6-month term. This is the key to WGU's value proposition - the faster you complete courses, the less you pay overall.

Federal Financial Aid (FAFSA)

WGU is a regionally accredited, nonprofit university that participates in all federal student aid programs. To qualify, complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at studentaid.gov.

Pell Grants

Pell Grants are free money from the federal government for undergraduate students with financial need. Key details:

  • Maximum award: $7,395 for 2024-2025 academic year
  • No repayment required - it's a grant, not a loan
  • Based on Expected Family Contribution (EFC) from FAFSA
  • Available for bachelor's degrees only (not graduate programs)

Federal Student Loans

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

If you work in public service (government, nonprofit, education, healthcare), you may qualify for loan forgiveness after 120 qualifying payments. WGU's teaching and nursing programs are particularly well-suited for PSLF.

Employer Tuition Assistance

This is the most underutilized benefit for working adults. Many employers offer tuition reimbursement programs, and WGU's $3,755/term cost often fits entirely within annual caps.

Common Employer Programs

  • Amazon Career Choice: 100% tuition covered at WGU for warehouse employees
  • Starbucks: Full tuition coverage through Arizona State partnership (but WGU may qualify under general education benefits)
  • Walmart: $1/day program for associates
  • Most Fortune 500 companies: $5,250/year tax-free benefit (IRS Section 127)
  • Healthcare employers: Often $3,000-$10,000/year for nursing programs

The key is asking your HR department. Many employees don't realize their company offers tuition assistance - or they assume online schools don't qualify. WGU's accreditation and nonprofit status means it qualifies for virtually all employer programs.

How to Maximize Employer Benefits

  1. Request your company's tuition assistance policy in writing
  2. Understand the requirements (GPA minimums, pre-approval process, service commitments)
  3. Time your enrollment to maximize reimbursement windows
  4. Accelerate your degree to complete within the reimbursement period

WGU Payment Options

If financial aid doesn't cover everything, WGU offers flexible payment options:

Monthly Payment Plan

Split your term cost into monthly payments with no interest. For a $3,755 term, that's approximately $626/month over 6 months.

Private Student Loans

If federal loans aren't enough, private loans can fill the gap. Compare rates from multiple lenders - WGU partners with several to offer competitive rates for students.

Out-of-Pocket Strategies

  • Use transfer credits to shorten your program - see our transfer credits guide
  • Accelerate aggressively to finish in fewer terms
  • Stack certifications you earn at WGU for immediate salary increases - check job outcome data

Military & Veterans Benefits

WGU is a Yellow Ribbon participant and one of the most military-friendly universities in the country. Benefits include:

  • Post-9/11 GI Bill: Full tuition coverage plus monthly housing allowance
  • Montgomery GI Bill: Monthly education benefit
  • Vocational Rehabilitation (VR&E): Chapter 31 benefits for disabled veterans
  • Military Tuition Assistance: Up to $250/credit hour while active duty
  • Yellow Ribbon Program: Additional funding beyond GI Bill caps

WGU's competency-based model is particularly valuable for veterans - military experience often translates to competencies that can be demonstrated through assessments, potentially shortening your time to degree.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does WGU cost per term?

WGU tuition ranges from $3,755 to $4,685 per 6-month term depending on your program. IT and Business programs are typically $3,755/term, while Nursing and some graduate programs cost more. Unlike traditional universities, you can complete unlimited courses within a term for the flat fee.

Does WGU accept FAFSA?

Yes, WGU is an accredited nonprofit university that participates in federal financial aid programs. You can apply using FAFSA and may qualify for Pell Grants, federal loans, and work-study programs. Apply at studentaid.gov using WGU's school code: 033394.

Can I get scholarships at WGU?

Yes, WGU offers numerous scholarships ranging from $2,000 to full tuition. Popular options include the WGU Loves Teachers Scholarship, Military Appreciation Scholarship, and state-specific scholarships. You can apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously through your WGU application portal.

How do I maintain financial aid eligibility?

You must maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP), which at WGU means completing at least 67% of attempted competency units and maintaining a passing rate. If you fall behind, you may be placed on financial aid warning or probation.

Can I use financial aid for certification exam fees?

Yes! For IT programs, certification exam vouchers are included in your tuition. Your financial aid covers the full cost of attendance, including these exams. See our certification guide for details on which certs are included.
