WGU vs Liberty University Online: Which Is Right for You?

January 4, 2026 9 min read Comparison

Choosing an online university often comes down to two major contenders: Western Governors University (WGU) and Liberty University Online (LUO). Both are non-profit, regionally accredited giants in the online education space, but they serve very different types of students.

The core difference? WGU is built for speed and autonomy, while Liberty offers structure and faith-based learning.

If you're trying to decide between the two, this guide breaks down tuition, learning models, and student experiences to help you pick the right path.

Quick Comparison: WGU vs Liberty

Feature Western Governors University (WGU) Liberty University Online (LUO)
Tuition Model Flat rate per 6-month term ($3,500 - $4,500) Per credit hour (~$390-$455) or block rate for full-time
Learning Style Competency-Based (Self-paced, no deadlines) Traditional (8-week terms, weekly deadlines)
Religious Affiliation Secular / Non-religious Christian (Biblical worldview integrated)
Acceleration Unlimited (Finish as fast as you can) Limited by 8-week course structure
Campus 100% Online (No physical campus) Online + Large physical campus in Virginia

WGU Overview: The Accelerator's Choice

WGU was created by 19 U.S. governors to modernize higher education. Its entire model revolves around Competency-Based Education (CBE). This means you don't spend time in class for things you already know.

How WGU Works

  • No Due Dates: You are given a 6-month term. As long as you finish your required courses by the end, when you finish them is up to you.
  • Pass/Fail Only: There are no GPAs. You either pass (proving competency) or you don't. A "Pass" is equivalent to a B grade (3.0).
  • All-You-Can-Eat Tuition: You pay one flat fee for 6 months. If you finish your required 4 courses in 2 months, you can add 4 more for free.
Best For

Self-disciplined students, career changers with existing knowledge, and those who want to graduate as fast and cheaply as possible.

Liberty University Overview: Structure & Faith

Liberty University is one of the largest Christian universities in the world. While they have a massive online program, they operate more like a traditional college.

How Liberty Online Works

  • 8-Week Terms: Courses are condensed into 8 weeks. You typically take 2 classes at a time.
  • Weekly Assignments: You will have discussion boards, papers, and quizzes due every week, usually on specific days (e.g., Thursday and Sunday).
  • Letter Grades: Liberty awards traditional letter grades (A, B, C), so you graduate with a GPA.
  • Christian Worldview: Every course, even math or IT, is taught from a biblical perspective. You will be required to take religion classes (e.g., Old/New Testament survey) as part of your degree.

Tuition Comparison: Which is Cheaper?

This is where the difference is starkest for accelerators.

WGU Cost

WGU charges roughly $4,000 per 6-month term. If you take 12 CUs (credits) or 60 CUs in that term, the price is the same. Learn more about accelerating here.

Liberty Cost

Liberty charges per credit hour. For undergraduate courses, it's often around $390 - $455 per credit hour part-time. If you go full-time, the rate drops significantly, but you are still paying per term in a way that limits "free" extra classes.

The Verdict:

  • If you take a standard load (12 credits/semester), costs are comparable.
  • If you accelerate (18+ credits/term), WGU is vastly cheaper.
  • If you need to take only 1 class at a time, Liberty might be cheaper (WGU requires full-time enrollment).

The Religious Aspect

This is a deal-breaker for some.

  • Liberty University: You will encounter Christianity. Assignments often ask you to integrate biblical principles. For believers, this is a huge plus. For secular students, it can feel like extra work or irrelevant content.
  • WGU: Completely secular. You study the subject matter, and that's it. No chapel, no required religion courses, no faith statements.

Accreditation & Reputation

Both universities are regionally accredited, which is the gold standard.

  • WGU: Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU).
  • Liberty: Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC).

Degrees from both are respected. WGU is often favored in tech and teaching sectors due to its skills-based focus. Liberty has a strong alumni network, especially in the South and in conservative circles.

Conclusion: Which One?

Choose WGU if:

  • You want to finish as fast as possible (1-2 years).
  • You are self-motivated and hate discussion boards.
  • You want the lowest possible cost for a degree.
  • You prefer a secular education.

Choose Liberty if:

  • You want a Christian education.
  • You need external structure (deadlines) to keep you on track.
  • You want a GPA (helpful for some competitive grad schools).
  • You prefer the traditional "class" feel with interaction.
