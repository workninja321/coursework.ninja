WGU IT Certifications: Which Certs Are Included and How to Pass Them

December 17, 2025 10 min read Degree Guides

One of WGU's biggest advantages is that industry certifications are built into your IT degree - and the exam vouchers are included in your tuition. Instead of paying separately for certifications that cost $300-$500 each, you'll earn valuable credentials while completing your degree.

This guide covers every certification included in WGU's IT programs, how the voucher system works, and proven strategies to pass these exams on your first attempt.

How WGU Certifications Work

WGU integrates industry certifications directly into specific courses. Here's how it works:

  1. Course preparation: Each certification-aligned course prepares you for the exam objectives
  2. Voucher provided: After completing the course material, you receive an exam voucher from your program mentor
  3. Two attempts included: Each voucher covers two exam attempts at no additional cost
  4. Pass to complete: Passing the certification exam is how you pass the course
Cost Savings: With WGU's $3,760 per term tuition, you could earn 5-9 certifications worth over $2,000 in exam fees alone - all included in your tuition.

CompTIA Certification Deep Dive

CompTIA certifications form the backbone of most WGU IT programs. Here's what you need to know about each:

CompTIA A+ (Core 1 & Core 2)

The A+ validates foundational IT skills and is often called the "entry-level IT certification." At WGU, it's split across two courses:

  • IT Foundations (Core 1): Hardware, mobile devices, networking basics
  • IT Applications (Core 2): Operating systems, security, troubleshooting

Difficulty: Moderate - most students with basic IT knowledge pass within 2-4 weeks of focused study per exam.

CompTIA Network+

Covers networking concepts, infrastructure, operations, security, and troubleshooting. This is a critical certification for IT careers.

Difficulty: Moderate to Hard - requires solid understanding of networking fundamentals. Budget 4-6 weeks of study.

CompTIA Security+

The most challenging CompTIA cert in most WGU programs. Covers threats, vulnerabilities, architecture, operations, and compliance.

Heads Up: Security+ is where many WGU students get stuck. It's a mile wide and an inch deep - meaning you need to know a little about many topics. Start studying early and use multiple resources.

Difficulty: Hard - plan for 6-10 weeks of dedicated study. Use Professor Messer videos, Jason Dion practice tests, and official CompTIA resources.

CompTIA Project+

Covers project management concepts including planning, execution, delivery, and closing. More business-focused than technical.

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate - many students pass this quickly, especially those with work experience in project environments.

Other Certifications

AWS Cloud Practitioner

An entry-level cloud certification that validates AWS knowledge. It's the easiest AWS cert and a great starting point for cloud careers.

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate - most students pass within 2-3 weeks with dedicated study using AWS Skill Builder (free) and practice exams.

ITIL Foundation

Covers IT service management best practices. This certification is valuable for IT management and operations roles.

Difficulty: Easy - mostly memorization of ITIL terminology and concepts. Most students pass within 1-2 weeks.

LPI Linux Essentials

Validates basic Linux knowledge including command line, file system, and open-source concepts.

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate - if you've used Linux before, this is straightforward. If not, budget 2-4 weeks to get comfortable with the command line.

Cisco CCNA

One of the most respected networking certifications. Only included in the Network Operations and Security program.

Difficulty: Hard - requires hands-on networking experience. Use Packet Tracer for labs and budget 8-12 weeks of study.

Strategies to Pass Certification Exams

Before You Start

  1. Review the exam objectives: Every certification has official objectives - make sure you can explain each one
  2. Gather resources: Don't rely solely on WGU materials. Use Professor Messer (free videos), Jason Dion courses, practice exams
  3. Schedule your exam early: Having a date creates accountability and prevents procrastination

Study Approach

  • Active recall: Use flashcards and practice tests instead of passive reading
  • Hands-on practice: For technical certs, lab practice is essential. Use virtual machines, Packet Tracer, or cloud free tiers
  • Practice exams: Take full-length practice tests under exam conditions. Aim for 85%+ before scheduling
  • Focus on weak areas: Identify gaps from practice tests and target those topics

Exam Day

  • Get good sleep the night before
  • Arrive early or ensure your testing environment is ready for online proctoring
  • Flag difficult questions and come back to them
  • Trust your preparation - don't second-guess yourself
