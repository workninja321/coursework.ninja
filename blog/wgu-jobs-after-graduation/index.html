What Employers Think of WGU Degrees

Perhaps the most important question: Do employers actually respect WGU degrees?

The Data Says Yes

According to Harris Poll surveys commissioned by WGU:

  • 100% of employers surveyed who hired WGU graduates were satisfied with their performance
  • 98% of graduates would recommend WGU to others
  • 97% of employers said WGU graduates met or exceeded expectations

Regional Accreditation Matters

WGU is regionally accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU). This is the same accreditation held by:

  • University of Washington
  • Stanford University
  • Oregon State University
  • Gonzaga University

Regional accreditation is the gold standard. It means WGU credits transfer to other regionally accredited institutions, and the degree is recognized by virtually all employers.

Pro Tip: When discussing your WGU degree with employers, mention the specific certifications you earned. IT certifications especially (CompTIA, Cisco, AWS) are highly valued and demonstrate practical skills.

Companies That Hire WGU Graduates

  • Finance: JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Goldman Sachs
  • Government: Department of Defense, VA, various federal and state agencies

Career Advancement Strategies

Getting the degree is step one. Here's how to maximize your career outcomes:

During Your Program

  • Earn certifications early: IT certifications included in your program are valuable before graduation
  • Update LinkedIn regularly: Add courses, certifications, and projects as you complete them
  • Network: Join WGU alumni groups and professional associations
  • Apply learned skills: Use coursework projects as portfolio pieces

Job Search Strategies

  • Highlight competency-based learning: Emphasize you proved mastery, not just attendance
  • Feature certifications prominently: These often matter more than the degree itself
  • Prepare to discuss your capstone: This demonstrates real project experience
  • Leverage WGU Career Services: Resume reviews, interview prep, and job boards

For tips on completing your degree efficiently, see our WGU Acceleration Guide and Study Tips for Working Adults.

After Graduation

  • Continue learning: WGU alumni get free access to certain resources
  • Maintain certifications: Keep your credentials current through continuing education
  • Consider graduate school: WGU credits transfer to other accredited institutions
  • Give back: Mentor current students, building your professional network

Addressing Common Concerns

"Online degrees aren't respected"

This outdated perception has largely disappeared. Post-COVID, employers understand online learning can be rigorous. WGU's competency-based model is often seen as more demanding because you must prove mastery, not just attend class.

"I won't have networking opportunities"

WGU offers virtual networking through alumni associations, cohorts, and professional communities. Many students find their professional networks grow through certification communities and industry events rather than college classmates.

"Employers won't know WGU"

With over 300,000 graduates, WGU is increasingly well-known. The university partners with major employers and is recognized as a top provider of diverse graduates in nursing, IT, and education.
