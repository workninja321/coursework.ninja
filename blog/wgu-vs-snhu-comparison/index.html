WGU vs SNHU: Which Online University Is Right for You?

Published: December 17, 2025 • 9 min read • Category: Comparisons

Choosing between Western Governors University (WGU) and Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU) is one of the most common dilemmas facing adult learners pursuing an online degree. Both are accredited, respected, and designed for working professionals, but they take fundamentally different approaches to education.

This comprehensive comparison will help you understand the key differences between WGU and SNHU so you can make an informed decision about your educational future.

The Fundamental Difference: Learning Models

The most significant difference between WGU and SNHU lies in their educational philosophy and structure.

WGU: Competency-Based Education

WGU pioneered competency-based education in higher education. Instead of attending scheduled classes for a set number of weeks, you demonstrate your knowledge through assessments. If you already understand the material, you can pass the assessment immediately and move on.

Key characteristics of WGU's model:

  • Self-paced learning: Move as fast or slow as you need
  • Flat-rate tuition: Pay per 6-month term, take unlimited courses
  • Assessment-focused: Prove mastery through Objective Assessments (OAs) and Performance Assessments (PAs)
  • Personal mentorship: Dedicated mentor guides your progress

SNHU: Traditional Online Education

SNHU uses a more traditional model adapted for online learning. Courses run on fixed 8-week terms with weekly assignments, discussions, and deadlines. You progress through a structured curriculum at a predetermined pace.

Key characteristics of SNHU's model:

  • Structured schedule: 8-week terms with clear deadlines
  • Per-credit pricing: Pay for each credit hour you take
  • Weekly engagement: Discussion boards, assignments, projects
  • Academic advising: Advisors help with course planning

Which Model Fits Your Learning Style?

If you're a self-motivated learner who can study independently and wants to accelerate through your degree, WGU's competency-based model could save you significant time and money. If you prefer structure, regular deadlines, and instructor guidance, SNHU's traditional model may be more comfortable.

Cost Comparison: WGU vs SNHU

Cost is often the deciding factor for adult learners. Here's how the two universities compare financially.

WGU Tuition Structure

WGU charges a flat rate per 6-month term:

  • IT programs: ~$4,085/term
  • Business programs: ~$3,755/term
  • Education programs: ~$3,755/term
  • Health programs: ~$4,530/term

The game-changer: you can complete as many courses as you can during each term at no extra cost. Motivated students regularly complete their degree in 1-2 years, dramatically reducing total costs.

SNHU Tuition Structure

SNHU charges per credit hour:

  • Online undergraduate: $320/credit hour
  • Online graduate: $627/credit hour
  • Bachelor's degree (120 credits): ~$38,400 total
  • Master's degree (36 credits): ~$22,572 total

Employer Recognition and Career Outcomes

A common concern is whether employers respect online degrees from these universities.

The Good News

Both WGU and SNHU are regionally accredited, which is the highest level of institutional accreditation in the United States. This means:

  • Credits transfer to other accredited institutions
  • Degrees qualify you for graduate programs
  • Employers recognize the credentials
  • Financial aid is available

Employer Partnerships

WGU has partnerships with major employers including:

  • Amazon
  • Google
  • Microsoft
  • Tenet Healthcare
  • Many state governments

SNHU has strong relationships with:

  • Major hospital systems
  • Fortune 500 companies
  • Military branches
  • Various state employers

The Reality

Your success after graduation depends far more on your skills, experience, and interview performance than which school you attended. Both degrees open doors; your performance keeps them open.

Who Should Choose WGU?

WGU is the better choice if you:

  • Are self-motivated and disciplined
  • Have prior knowledge in your field
  • Want to finish faster and save money
  • Value certifications (especially IT)
  • Prefer assessment-based learning
  • Can study independently without much structure
  • Want flexibility in your daily schedule

Who Should Choose SNHU?

SNHU is the better choice if you:

  • Prefer structured deadlines
  • Want a traditional college experience online
  • Value peer interaction and discussions
  • Are pursuing liberal arts or creative degrees
  • Are active military (strong tuition discount)
  • Need more instructor guidance
  • Prefer predictable pacing

Making Your Decision

The "right" choice depends entirely on your personal circumstances, learning style, and goals. Consider these questions:

  1. How much time can you dedicate? If you can commit 20+ hours/week, WGU's acceleration potential is valuable.
  2. What's your budget? If cost is the primary concern, WGU typically wins, especially with acceleration.
  3. What program do you need? Some programs are only available at one school.
  4. How do you learn best? Self-directed learners thrive at WGU; those needing structure prefer SNHU.
  5. What's your timeline? Need to finish fast? WGU. Prefer a steady pace? SNHU.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is WGU or SNHU easier to graduate from?

Neither university is inherently "easier." WGU uses competency-based education where you advance by demonstrating mastery through assessments. SNHU uses traditional term-based courses with weekly assignments. WGU may be faster for self-motivated learners with prior knowledge, while SNHU offers more structure for those who prefer guided pacing.

Which is cheaper: WGU or SNHU?

WGU is generally more affordable, especially if you accelerate. WGU charges approximately $3,755-$4,530 per 6-month term (unlimited courses), while SNHU charges around $320 per credit hour ($38,400 for a 120-credit bachelor's). At WGU, completing your degree faster directly reduces total cost.

Do employers prefer WGU or SNHU?

Both universities are regionally accredited and respected by employers. WGU has partnerships with major employers and is particularly well-regarded in IT and healthcare. SNHU has strong name recognition and is respected across industries. Your skills, experience, and interview performance matter more than which school you attended.

Can I transfer credits between WGU and SNHU?

Yes, both universities accept transfer credits from accredited institutions. WGU is generous with transfer credits and also accepts industry certifications. SNHU accepts up to 90 transfer credits for bachelor's degrees. Contact admissions at either school for a personalized transfer credit evaluation.

The Bottom Line

Both WGU and SNHU are legitimate, accredited universities that have helped hundreds of thousands of adult learners earn their degrees. The "better" choice is the one that fits your learning style, budget, and goals.

If you value speed, flexibility, and saving money, WGU's competency-based model is hard to beat. If you prefer structure, weekly deadlines, and a more traditional college experience, SNHU may be your better fit.

Whichever path you choose, the most important thing is to start. Your future self will thank you for taking this step toward your educational and career goals.
