WGU Course Order Strategy: The Best Sequence to Take Your Classes

December 17, 2025 12 min read Study Tips

One of the most powerful advantages of WGU's competency-based model is the freedom to take courses in any order you choose—within prerequisite constraints. But with great freedom comes great responsibility. The order in which you tackle your courses can mean the difference between blazing through your degree in record time or struggling with burnout and frustration.

This guide reveals the strategic principles successful WGU students use to sequence their courses for maximum momentum, knowledge retention, and acceleration.

Why Course Order Actually Matters

You might think that since WGU is self-paced and competency-based, course order shouldn't matter much. But successful accelerators will tell you it matters more than you think:

  • Knowledge building: Some courses provide foundational concepts that make later courses easier
  • Momentum creation: Starting with wins builds confidence and study habits
  • Cognitive efficiency: Batching related courses reduces context-switching overhead
  • Assessment timing: Strategic sequencing avoids grading bottlenecks with PAs
  • Energy management: Spacing out difficult courses prevents burnout

Poor course sequencing can add weeks or even months to your degree completion time, while strategic ordering can help you accelerate through WGU faster than you thought possible.

Understanding Prerequisites and Required Sequences

Before planning your ideal order, you need to know which courses must be taken in sequence. WGU enforces certain prerequisites that cannot be overridden:

Hard Prerequisites

These are absolute requirements programmed into the system:

  • Foundational courses: General education or core courses often must come first
  • Sequential classes: Calculus I before Calculus II, for example
  • Certification stacks: Some IT certs must be earned in order
  • Capstone requirements: Usually requires completion of all or most other courses

Soft Prerequisites

These aren't system-enforced but are highly recommended:

  • Conceptual building blocks: Statistics before data analysis courses
  • Skill development: Basic programming before advanced software courses
  • Writing progression: Composition before research-heavy PAs
Pro Tip: Work with your Program Mentor to get a complete prerequisite map for your degree program. This prevents planning sequences that won't work when it's time to register.

The Power of Batching Related Courses

One of the most effective acceleration strategies is course batching—grouping similar courses together to maximize knowledge transfer and minimize cognitive switching costs.

Why Batching Works

  • Shared vocabulary: Related courses use the same terminology and concepts
  • Overlapping content: Material from one course reinforces another
  • Mental momentum: Staying in one subject area keeps you in "flow state"
  • Resource efficiency: Study materials often apply to multiple courses

Effective Batching Examples

IT Programs:

  • Group all networking courses together (Network+, CCNA, Network Design)
  • Batch security courses (Security+, Cybersecurity, Ethical Hacking)
  • Take all cloud courses in sequence (AWS, Azure concepts)

Business Programs:

  • Complete all accounting courses consecutively
  • Batch finance and economics courses
  • Group marketing and strategy courses

Nursing Programs:

  • Batch anatomy and physiology courses
  • Group pharmacology and pathophysiology
  • Take leadership courses together
Important: Don't batch too many difficult courses together. Mix challenging courses with easier ones to prevent burnout and maintain steady progress.

OA Courses vs PA Courses: Strategic Sequencing

WGU courses are assessed through either Objective Assessments (exams) or Performance Assessments (written assignments). Each type requires different sequencing strategies:

OA Course Characteristics

  • Immediate results after completion
  • Can be scheduled and taken on demand
  • Pass/fail determined instantly
  • Better for building quick momentum

PA Course Characteristics

  • 3-5 business day grading turnaround
  • May require revisions and resubmission
  • Time-intensive to produce original work
  • Can create bottlenecks if not planned carefully

Optimal OA/PA Sequencing Strategy

Start of Term:

  1. Begin with 2-3 OA courses you're confident about for quick wins
  2. Start your first PA early so it's grading while you work on OAs
  3. Submit PAs by Tuesday or Wednesday to avoid weekend grading delays

Mid-Term:

  1. Alternate between OAs and PAs to maintain momentum
  2. Always have a PA "in the pipeline" being graded
  3. Use PA grading time to knock out OA pre-assessments

End of Term:

  1. Front-load PAs so you're not waiting for grades at the deadline
  2. Save a couple of easy OA courses for the final week as backup
  3. Avoid submitting critical PAs too close to term end

Starting With What You Already Know

The fastest path through any WGU degree is identifying and capitalizing on your existing knowledge. This requires assessment before action:

The Pre-Assessment Survey Strategy

  1. Take all pre-assessments: At the start of your term, take pre-assessments for every available course
  2. Identify high scorers: Note which courses you scored 75%+ on without any study
  3. Schedule these first: Courses where you're already competent should be your initial targets
  4. Quick certification: Take the actual assessment within days of the pre-assessment
Pro Tip: Many students discover they can pass certain courses immediately based on work experience. One IT student passed 6 networking courses in his first two weeks by leveraging his existing knowledge.

Leveraging Work Experience

Match courses to your professional background:

  • Accountants: Knock out finance and accounting courses first
  • IT professionals: Clear certification courses aligned with your role
  • Managers: Complete business management and leadership courses early
  • Healthcare workers: Start with clinical courses in your specialty

Building Momentum With Quick Wins

The psychological importance of early momentum cannot be overstated. Starting your term with quick wins creates a positive feedback loop that carries you through harder courses:

Identifying Quick Win Courses

  • Check WGU Reddit: r/WGU has crowd-sourced difficulty ratings for every course
  • Ask your cohort: Course chatter reveals which courses students finish fastest
  • Pre-assessment scores: High scores indicate potential quick completions
  • Credit units: Lower CU courses (2-3 credits) often move faster

The First Week Blitz

Successful accelerators often complete 2-4 courses in their first week by targeting quick wins. This strategy:

  • Establishes confidence in the WGU system
  • Proves to yourself that acceleration is possible
  • Creates buffer room for harder courses later
  • Impresses your Program Mentor and unlocks more flexibility

Saving Capstones and Difficult Courses

Not all courses are created equal. Some are universally recognized as challenging and time-intensive. Strategic timing of these courses is critical:

Why Save Difficult Courses for Later

  • Skill development: Earlier courses build skills you'll need for harder ones
  • Study habits: You'll have refined your WGU study system by mid-term
  • Momentum protection: Starting with hard courses risks early burnout
  • Knowledge accumulation: Comprehensive courses benefit from prior learning

The Capstone Strategy

Capstone projects are the crown jewel of your degree—comprehensive, time-intensive, and designed to integrate everything you've learned. Almost universally, you should:

  • Complete last: Save capstone for your final course when knowledge is maximized
  • Start planning early: Begin thinking about capstone topics in earlier courses
  • Gather resources: Collect relevant materials throughout your program
  • Allow extra time: Don't plan to rush capstone completion

Program-Specific Recommendations

Different WGU programs require different sequencing strategies. Here are proven approaches for major programs:

BS Information Technology (BSIT)

  1. Start with: CompTIA A+ if you have IT experience
  2. Early focus: Knock out all CompTIA certifications (Network+, Security+)
  3. Batch: Group all networking courses together
  4. Mid-program: Tackle scripting and programming courses
  5. Save for later: Advanced certifications like ITIL or project management
  6. Final course: Capstone project

Learn more in our detailed WGU BSIT Guide.

BS Computer Science

  1. Start with: General education courses if no CS background
  2. Foundation: Intro to programming and discrete math early
  3. Build skills: Progress through programming courses sequentially
  4. Mid-program: Data structures and algorithms (challenging but essential)
  5. Advanced: Software engineering and system design
  6. Final: Computer science capstone

More details in our WGU Computer Science Degree Guide.

MBA

  1. Start with: Courses aligned with your work experience
  2. Early: Marketing, operations, or finance based on your background
  3. Batch: Financial courses together, then management courses
  4. Mid-MBA: Strategy and leadership courses
  5. Final two terms: Electives that interest you, then capstone

See our WGU MBA Complete Guide for comprehensive details.

Nursing Programs (BSN, MSN)

  1. Foundation: General education and anatomy/physiology
  2. Core clinical: Patient care and assessment courses
  3. Specialization: Courses in your area of nursing focus
  4. Advanced: Pharmacology and pathophysiology
  5. Leadership: Management and leadership in nursing
  6. Final: Clinical capstone or practicum

General Strategy Across Programs

  • Term 1: Easy wins + courses matching your background
  • Term 2: Core program courses in logical batches
  • Term 3+: Advanced courses with strategic difficulty distribution
  • Final term: Capstone and any remaining difficult courses

Using Pre-Assessments to Determine Order

Pre-assessments are your secret weapon for optimizing course order. They reveal exactly where you stand on each competency:

The Pre-Assessment Process

  1. Take them all: Complete pre-assessments for every available course at term start
  2. Review coaching reports: Understand which competencies need work
  3. Create a priority matrix: Sort courses by readiness level
  4. Schedule accordingly: Highest readiness courses go first

Pre-Assessment Score Interpretation

  • 75%+ (Proficient): Schedule the OA immediately—you may already know this
  • 60-74% (Approaching): Quick study target—probably 1-3 days of focused review
  • 40-59% (Basic): Moderate effort needed—1-2 weeks of study
  • Below 40%: Significant study required—save for later or batch with related courses
Pro Tip: Don't be discouraged by low pre-assessment scores. They're diagnostic tools, not predictors of final performance. Many students score poorly on pre-assessments and pass the actual exam after focused study.

Dynamic Re-Ordering

Use pre-assessments to adjust your plan mid-term:

  • If a planned "hard" course shows high pre-assessment scores, move it up
  • If an "easy" course shows low readiness, push it back for more preparation
  • Constantly reevaluate based on new information
