Understanding the WGU Capstone Challenge

The capstone project is the culminating academic experience of your WGU degree program. It's designed to demonstrate that you can apply everything you've learned to a comprehensive, real-world project. Unlike regular Performance Assessments that focus on individual competencies, capstones integrate multiple competencies into a single, substantial deliverable.

Why Capstones Are So Challenging

20-50+ pages of original research. Exacting rubrics that must be satisfied point by point. Graduate-level methodology and citations. All while balancing work and family. This is the final barrier between you and your degree.

We understand why students struggle. After months or years of balancing responsibilities, you're asked to produce a research project that would challenge a full-time student. You may have never written anything of this length or complexity.

That's Why We Exist

Our capstone specialists have completed hundreds of WGU capstones across every program. We know what evaluators look for, what causes revisions, and how to craft projects that pass on the first submission.

WGU Capstone Types by Program

WGU capstones vary significantly by program. Understanding your specific capstone requirements is essential to completing it successfully. Here's an overview of the major capstone types we support.

MBA Capstone

The MBA capstone requires you to conduct a strategic analysis of a real organization and develop actionable recommendations for improvement. This typically involves:

  • Selecting an organization (your employer or a company you research)
  • Analyzing the company's strategic position using frameworks like SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, or PESTEL
  • Identifying specific problems or opportunities
  • Developing detailed recommendations with implementation plans
  • Supporting all analysis with data and proper citations

The MBA capstone spans multiple tasks: a proposal (Task 1), the main strategic analysis paper (Task 2), and often a presentation or executive summary (Task 3). Our MBA specialists have completed hundreds of these projects across industries including healthcare, technology, retail, manufacturing, and professional services.

MBA Task 1: Proposal

Topic selection, organizational context, problem statement, research questions, methodology outline.

MBA Task 2: Strategic Analysis

15-30 page paper with external/internal analysis, recommendations, implementation timeline, financial projections.

IT Capstone Projects

WGU's College of IT offers various capstone types depending on your specific degree program:

Software Development Capstone

Requires designing and developing a functional software application that solves a real-world problem. Includes documentation of requirements, design decisions, implementation, and testing. Our developers have completed projects ranging from web applications to mobile apps to data analytics platforms.

Cybersecurity Capstone

Involves conducting a security assessment, developing security policies, or designing secure network architectures. Requires demonstrating understanding of threats, vulnerabilities, controls, and compliance frameworks. Our cybersecurity experts hold relevant certifications and industry experience.

Data Analytics Capstone

Focuses on collecting, analyzing, and presenting data to solve a business problem. Requires proper statistical methodology, visualization, and actionable insights. Our data analysts work with tools like Python, R, Tableau, and SQL to create compelling analytical projects.

IT Management Capstone

Centers on IT project management, strategic planning, or organizational technology assessment. Requires understanding of both technical concepts and business implications. Our specialists bridge the technical-business gap effectively.

What's Included in Our Capstone Service

Topic Development

Choosing the right topic is critical. A poor topic choice can doom your capstone before you even begin. We help you select topics that are:

  • Viable within your timeline and resources
  • Appropriately scoped (not too broad, not too narrow)
  • Aligned with program requirements

Proposal/Prospectus (Task 1)

The proposal sets the foundation for your entire capstone. We craft proposals that:

  • Clearly articulate the problem or opportunity
  • Present viable research questions or project objectives
  • Outline appropriate methodology
  • Demonstrate understanding of relevant literature
  • Set realistic scope and timeline expectations
  • Address all rubric requirements for Task 1

Getting the proposal approved on the first attempt saves significant time and prevents downstream issues.

Main Project Execution (Task 2)

This is the heart of your capstone—the comprehensive document that demonstrates your mastery. Our experts:

Presentation Materials (Task 3)
